Kishkinda Kanda

 

Prelude:

Rama meets Sugriva and forges a friendship true

He kills Vali and gets Sugriva his kingdom and wife: his due

Sugriva assembles his army and a search for Sita he launches

Rama hands over his ring to Hanuman, who fear and misery staunches 

Monkeys sent in all directions except the south, return empty-handed

Hanuman's group reach the southern ocean shore, and learn at Lanka Sita is stranded.

 

 

The story in detail:

 

As Rama and Lakshmana neared the Rishyamukha mountain

The vigilant Sugriva watched them with a mounting tension

Swiftly calling his minister Hanuman’s attention

Sugriva said: “I alternate between hope and consternation.

Perceive the two warriors that eagerly approach

Their erect and noble aspect is beyond reproach.”

 

“I charge you to go forward and find

Whether they are friend or foe: what kind

In case they are emissaries of Vali, my brother cruel

You may count me among the dead without even a duel

If they are men with intent neutral

I can befriend them and to my advantage use their arrival.”

 

Hanuman, that loyal monkey, fully endowed

Prepared to spy: into their midst, can the new entrants be allowed?

“I shall transform into a ‘Brahmin’ and accost the two

I will gather their purpose and soon make my report to you,”

Said he with earnest zeal and made for the way

Along which Rama-Lakshmana walked, making rapid headway. 

 

Hanuman was the son of ‘Anjana’ and ‘Vayu’, the wind god

Christened Maruti, his charm and strength, everyone did laud

One day a ravenous baby ‘Anjaneya’ went for the sun

Thinking it to be a delicious fruit that must be won.

The frightened sun god ran to Indra and sought refuge

The enraged King of gods struck the baby like a deluge

 

Hit near the mouth by Indra’s thunderbolt, Maruti fell to earth

Unconscious and with a swollen jaw, lay that baby of infinite worth

Vayu, angered, held his breath and refused to let go

All created beings began to choke as a result of this row

The anxious Indra and Lord Brahma hastened forward

To mollify Vayu and set right the worlds' suffering, untoward

 

Lord Brahma said, “O Vayu, please fulfil your function

Do not torment the world; have some compunction.” 

The wrathful Vayu asked, “Indra has attacked

My son, a mere child: is his act being backed?”

Brahma pacifically replied, “Indra is here to make Maruti well

He agrees, his indignation, he should have worked to quell.”

 

At this, Indra apologized profusely and begged Vayu for pardon:

 “Maruti reached for the sun thinking it to be a fruit from a garden

On beholding his act, I was gripped by a thoughtless panic 

And not considering his playful ignorance, I gave in to a fury volcanic

Maruti will acquire fame as ‘Hanuman’ the one with a broken jaw

Look at him rise now; with his intelligence, everyone he shall draw.”

 

Pacified, Vayu let out the wind force he had been holding

He hugged Hanuman to his chest with his love unfolding

Every god in heaven came forth and bestowed boons

On Hanuman, the wonder child, with the strength of typhoons

Lord Brahma gave Maruti a blessing most valuable:

“You shall recognise the ‘Paramatma’: my boon is infallible.”

 

Everyone present was overjoyed for Maruti, that prodigious little child 

Who asked, “How do I understand I’ve seen the supreme?” with a smile mild 

Brahma explained, “Realise you are in the Supreme’s presence

When your bones melt and love infinite fills your every sense.”

Hanuman thanked all the gods congregated in the assembly

A celibate and forever Rama’s ardent devotee, he was born to be.

 

That remarkable Hanuman now appeared before Sri Rama and looked

He felt his bones thawing and his every sense becoming hooked

He knew he was seeing the Lord of Lords Himself in person

And realised his life had been sanctified beyond comparison

The tears began to flow down the face of that master of self-control:

Is it possible for a devotee to keep his senses under strict patrol?

 

Hanuman, in the presence of Rama, the Pure Soul

Failed to conceal his identity, though that was his goal

Upon Hanuman, Rama's cool and all-bestowing gaze fell 

The monkey's sins fled and nothing but truth could he spell!

"Who are you two, who resemble beings from heaven?

Though dressed like gentle hermits, you look far from craven."

 

"You have a bold stance, rounded shoulders, and broad chest

Warriors you must be: that, your features do suggest

Why have you left your well-shaped bodies bare?

To don jewels that offer cover from evil glances, you should’ve taken care.

I am Hanuman, son of Vayu and minister of Sugriva, the exiled monkey King

Details about the two of you, he directed me to bring."

 

Rama listened to Hanuman's speech with a growing admiration

"Tell him about us, dear Lakshmana," He said with determination 

"Had the monkey chief spoken to me, I would have replied

Since his minister has spoken, through you, my response must be supplied

I have delegated to you the position of my dear, departed father

In this far-away forest, I charge you to also act as my envoy and minister."

 

"Let me warn you to be careful, before you begin to speak:

Your chances of exceeding Hanuman's articulation are but weak

Not only were his words, tone and diction faultless

Even his body language and facial expressions were fabulous.

His mouth opened and shut just like graceful lotus petals

His speech, clear and concise, is worthy of earning medals."

 

"With such an assistant by his side, no king needs to worry

Easily accomplishing formidable feats, he can make merry

This son of Vayu has exhibited the nuances of political wisdom

His support would mean security and growth to any kingdom

Evidently, Hanuman is a monkey most remarkably learned 

He must know the nine grammars of 'Sanskrit' and the  'Veda-Vedanta', sacred."

 

Coming from the 'Vaagme Sriman' Rama, this was indeed high praise

For, Rama's eloquence had won accolades from Vasishta, hard to entice.

Lakshmana, the avatar of Adisesha, the thousand-hooded serpent

Had a thousand tongues to speak with, to everyone's content

That Rama should caution this capable speaker

Is proof of Hanuman's infinite intelligence and sagacity proper

 

Rama further said: "From learning the Rig Veda, Hanuman has acquired real humility

Yajur and Sama Veda have given him retentive powers and scholarly temerity.

I feel a new confidence on account of this providential meeting

I also wish to acquaint with Sugriva and offer him my greeting.

Once again Lakshmana, think through what you want to say, well

Speak to Hanuman and all his doubts, please dispel."

 

Now, Lakshmana began to narrate their story in brief:

"We are sons of Dasaratha, Kosala's emperor and an Ikshvaku chief

To keep his word, he sent his eldest son Rama on a fourteen-year exile

I, Rama's brother, nay slave, and Sita, His wife, followed upon His trail

After spending thirteen years, meeting scores of ascetics in peace

We were visited by a misfortune: Sita, the demon Ravana did seize."

 

"This here is Rama: together we have come with the sole desire

To seek and rescue Sita, for which a friend we require."

Listening intently to Lakshmana's narrative, 

Hanuman came to a conclusion definitive

"Permit me to take you to Sugriva, shunned by Vali, his brother

You would benefit mutually: of this, I am absolutely sure."

 

Hanuman led Rama and Lakshmana to Sugriva's cave

And a sacred and influential friendship he went on to pave

Said Hanuman: "O Sugriva, behold Rama, who resembles a fearless lion

O Rama, here is powerful Sugriva, the sun god's scion

Please extend and clasp your hands in friendship 

Pledge to destroy your unjust enemies with this strong partnership."

 

Sugriva and Rama did as Hanuman suggested

And burning a fire as their holy witness, the two united

Sitting side by side, tied in friendship's true and strong bond

Rama and Sugriva gazed at each other for long, with smiles fond

Just then the left eyes of Vali and Ravana quivered, portending their defeat

And the left eye of Sita too throbbed, foretelling an auspicious treat.

 

Presently, Sugriva brought forth a piece of cloth tied into a bundle

And asked: "Do this cloth and the jewels inside, any memories, kindle?

Many days back we saw a lady being taken along the aerial route

Amidst us, jewels wrapped in her upper cloth, she did shoot

Do they belong to your dear wife Sita—please verify

By helping you win Her back, I shall our friendship, fructify."

 

Rama, upon recognising Sita's garment, became tearful

Riotous emotions gripped Him and made His aspect fearful

Asking Lakshmana to identify the bundle, Rama brooded 

Sugriva opened the cloth that with Sita's jewels was loaded

He pulled out the ornaments one by one and exhibited each

Lakshmana said, "O Sugriva, show me the anklets alone, I beseech."

 

"I have never gazed on my sister-in-law, out of modesty and deference,

I have seen Her anklets though, when bowing at her feet daily as a mark of reverence 

I can confirm this pair belongs to that gentle lady of peerless character 

Please narrate to us what transpired and describe the cruel abductor."

In reply, Sugriva said, "We saw a goddess-like lady being borne away

She looked terrified, with her face bathed in tears and her hair in disarray."

 

However, when she perceived us, this package she did fling

With folded hands, solace, she prayed the heavens to bring."

Hearing this, the equanimous Rama was moved to a terrible rage

Sugriva, in a soft voice, consoled Him and advised courage

"After You eliminate Vali, I shall command our monkey army, vast

We will find Sita: I will not let your grief last."

 

Pacified by the soothing counsel, Rama soon became composed

He asked Sugriva, "Describe well why Vali towards you is ill-disposed."

Sugriva answered: "Vali, my elder, ruled over Kishkinda wisely

His power is so great that none would fight him wantonly

But one day, a demon named Dundubi challenged him to a combat

And Vali rushed into battle, desiring to crush him like a rat.

 

For many days, the two continued to fight

But, there came a day when Dundubi could not meet Vali's might

The demon took to his heels and hid in a cave

Vali followed and said to me: "I shall make this place his grave.

You guard the mouth of this cave until I return

I do not want Dundubi to escape and from the fight adjourn."

 

Thus instructing me, Vali went after Dundubi with a fearsome roar

A year went by, but I continued waiting near the door

Soon after, though, I heard frantic and desperate cries

Following this, blood gushed out: I couldn't believe my eyes

"O my poor brother is dead," I thought and was by grief blinded

"I must shut the cave—here, the demon must be grounded."

 

Rolling a huge boulder to the cave's mouth, I carefully sealed it

And running back to our kingdom, I lamented: "Mighty Vali is hit.

My brother lives no more, we are without a guide

Is there anyone to replace Vali, our tribe's pride?"

Soon all the monkey elders came together and decided

"You ascend the throne and safeguard it": thus was I goaded.

 

Vali, however, had not died and soon he made an appearance

When he saw I had been crowned King, he  lost all his coherence

"You tried shutting me up in the cave, you coward! 

But, I have obliterated your plan, vile and soured.

Now, I shall kill you with my hands bare

For your dastardly act, your life I shall not spare."

 

Thus swore Vali and I trembled in despair and trepidation 

Trying to pacify him, I ventured into a rapid explanation 

Vali, not inclined to believe me, came at me with raised arms

I, while fleeing him, continued to placate him with folded palms

But, that cruel brother of mine ignored all my pleas and submission

To crush me into oblivion seemed his one and only mission.

 

"I took refuge in this mountain, where Vali dares not enter

He has been cursed: if he sets foot here, his head shall shatter

I have resided here since then, with barely a semblance of dignity

Vali took over my wife as his own, showing further his malignity

Hanuman and a few more monkeys who to me are faithful

Live with me and of Vali's whereabouts we ever remain watchful."

 

All this Sugriva said in a subdued and trembling tone 

Rama knew, Vali's excesses, He could not condone

He said, "You and I have been separated forcibly from our wives

To annihilate the criminals, you watch how your friend Rama strives

You go to Vali's gates and challenge him to a duel

With my sharp arrow, I shall pierce his heart cruel."

 

Sugriva, though pleased, said: "I can describe Vali's brawn by the hour.

Vali's back and Vishnu's ‘Kurma avatar’, have the same power

The nails on Vali's fingers match the strength of ‘Narasimha's nails

At the vigour of Vali's teeth that match ‘Varaha's teeth, the entire world quails

My brother Vali has vitality that equals a multitude of gods

People who try to underrate his brute force are certainly retards."

 

While Sugriva praised his brother, Rama did not interrupt:

He wanted to eradicate his friend's doubts without being abrupt

With calm assurance He said: "Using my unparalleled bow,

I shoot the most potent arrows that suppress any foe.

So, go forth and call out to Vali, that unreasonable sinner

I shall kill him and you can reclaim your wife, as a winner."

 

Even after Rama's assertion, Sugriva remained unconvinced 

Hanuman, who knew Rama for the Supreme, inwardly winced 

He took Sugriva aside and counselled, "You could conduct a test.

Then, Rama's capability, you could for yourself attest.

I recognise Rama as the Lord of all creation

His mere will can nullify Vali: know Rama as Narayana's incarnation."

 

So, Sugriva went up to Rama and spoke thoughtfully

"Do you perceive those massive sal trees grown fully?

Vali can shoot each one down with a single stroke!

Can you do that my friend: excuse my doubts, if they provoke!"

Rama got up and straightening His bow shot an arrow

Instantly, it pierced seven sal trees and returned to His quiver, in one go.

 

Sugriva, though elated, had one more test on his mind

He said, "Yonder lies Dundubi's skeleton, a colossus of his kind

This carcass Vali had thrown many miles, after killing the fiend

Will You show us what You can do? With that my doubts would end!"

Rama, with His ever-present smile, turned to Lakshmana and gestured

That exemplar kicked the skeleton and it flew into oblivion, unfettered.

 

Sugriva, his reservations vanquished, squared his shoulders 

And strode forth to challenge Vali, capable of smashing boulders

He went to the gates of Kishkinda and roared, "Vali, you coward

I challenge you to a duel: if you dare, you must come forward."

Sugriva then smacked his shoulders and thighs in readiness 

While Vali, though rudely shaken, became eager to show his rowdiness.

 

Tara, Vali’s discerning wife, stopped him and warned:

“Sugriva has joined forces with Rama, who is not to be scorned.

When all these days, he has been cowering at your very name

You must think wherefrom he gets this courage without blame.

Rama is not an ordinary mortal—I have heard of his deeds 

He is both valorous and sublime and He always succeeds.”

 

Vali heard his wife’s counsel but refused to sit tight

“I do agree with what you say, but cannot ignore a challenge to my might

I would rather die a heroic death than remain craven

Have faith in your Vali’s strength and for my victory, pray to heaven.”

Saying this, Vali hurried out of his palace with his mace

And Tara looked on, troubled over what was to take place.

 

While Sugriva and Vali assembled to duel unto death

Rama hid behind a tree, ready to strike an arrow into Vali’s formidable girth

But, when he saw the two great apes fighting one another

Their close physical resemblance perplexed Him altogether

Reluctant to let go of His arrow, Rama stood mesmerised

While by Vali poor Sugriva was getting pulverised

 

Soon, unable to keep up with Vali’s pugnacious instinct

Sugriva's fear and regret over entering the duel became distinct

Deciding to quit the fight, Sugriva fled at top speed:

The derisive Vali's taunts hurt him like a poisonous weed

Coming over to Rama, he cried, "Oh, why for me this humiliation?

While your friend was being badgered, why did you pause in hesitation?"

 

Rama hurriedly explained: "I failed to distinguish you from your brother

I refrained from shooting, thinking a wrong target I might smother

Here, wear this garland of leaves so that I may recognise you 

Now, go and challenge Vali once more, so I can give him his due."

Sugriva placed implicit trust in Rama and obeyed his words

He went out to Vali and for a duel, roared again from his innards.

 

Vali, astonished beyond belief, returned in a rush to fight

And shouted, "You have asked for death as your plight."

This time, Rama made no delay in releasing His arrow, sure

It found Vali's heart and pierced it, wounding him without cure

Lying prostrate on the ground, Vali looked around for his attacker

Only a powerful divinity could slay him in this way, instanter.

 

Vali now perceived Rama emerging from behind a clump of trees

And felt His pleasing form caress him like a soothing breeze

Sugriva sprang down at Vali's feet and out of guilt sobbed

"My own brother, I have killed," cried he, while his heart in sorrow throbbed

Rama walked up and placed His hand on Sugriva's shoulder, pacifically 

And He too began to lament over His friend's loss, brokenly 

 

Excruciatingly, Vali pulled out the fatal arrow from his chest

He recognised it as coming from Rama's quiver by its crest

Shocked, he asked Rama, "Oh Rama, You stand there like a lovely painting 

Your handsome countenance and soft eyes are so misleading

You are like a dry well whose mouth is covered with leaves: 

Anyone who walks over it, it sucks in and cleaves."

 

How could you hit me, concealed behind a tree?

Being Dasaratha's son, I never could dream this of thee

Wait: even that King sent You into exile as per a woman's whim

But, being Bharatha's brother, how could you in malpractice swim?

Separated from Your virtuous wife, who came out of the milky ocean

You know not what You do; You have lost Your compassion."

 

Vali's words, though uttered in anger and disappointment, held a great truth

Sita's proximity and distance determined Rama's pity and ruth 

When She is near Him, He condones and never kills

But, when Sita is away, Rama punishes using His warring skills

Sita is tolerance-and-forgiveness personified 

To practise these qualities, She constantly keeps Rama notified.

 

Vali continued: "Did Your ancestor Manu, who laid down Dharmic rules, state:

'To recover his kidnapped wife, a monkey, one must kill and cremate?'

Then, the sarcastic Vali proceeded to ask of Rama six questions straight

He meant to understand Rama's justification for contradicting His renowned trait

"You and I do not have troubles over our kingdoms' borders

We know not each other nor are we enemies to pass killing orders."

 

"While I am part of the animal species, You are a human, noble

Assuming You had reason to kill me, concealing Yourself was surely ignoble?"

If You came to hunt me: did You crave my skin, hair, nails, or meat?

If You punished me: did I, a Brahmin, woman or a King, murder or beat?

Answer me these questions with truth and sincerity

If you fail to convince me, You shall lose Your fame for eternity."

 

Rama listened to Vali's words with His usual smile, divine and calm

Standing—bent at the knee, waist and neck—He cast forth a divine charm.

Rama then began to answer Vali's questions with quiet confidence:

After all, of valour, mercy and justice, He was the supreme confluence 

Vali, despite being in death's throes, listened with rapt attention

And felt mollified by Rama's soothing tones that held silent conviction 

 

Rama said: “Bharatha rules from Ayodhya; I’m here as his servant:

That I ensure peace in the jungle regions is Bharatha’s desire fervent

This entire earth belongs to the Ikshvaku dynasty 

That Kishkinda is under you is your impression hasty

Hence I do take issue with you on the sharing of land 

As an answer to your first question, this to you, I hand.”

 

“There is no need to know each other for discord to rise

And this answers your second question too, without compromise

I remained unseen: while hunting an animal, it is the norm

I came to hunt you, because to fair behaviour you did not conform

By taking your brother's wife forcibly for your own 

Into the chasm of despicable misconduct did you drown.”

 

Understand that I hunted you down as punishment:

Thus have I answered your doubts with no need for concealment.”

Upon hearing Rama's exposition, Vali felt vastly satisfied:

"Your justice is clear and not disputable," he sighed

“O Rama, how big Your heart is to answer an inferior and sinner!

By Your gracious glance alone, I shall reach Your permanent realm, a winner."

 

“After You rescue Sita and return to Ayodhya to assume sovereignty

Your brothers will ask You to describe Your experiences, in all probability

When You tell them, “Sugriva got me to kill Vali, his sibling”

They may mock my brother’s hostility without thinking

Please stop them and say: “Sugriva accepted Kishkinda, a place insignificant

And gave Vali a berth at Sri Vaikunta, that abode supreme and permanent.”

 

“Protect Sugriva as Your friend forever and also Angada, my young son

Always at Your side, keep Hanuman, the monkey who cannot be undone.”

Saying this, Vali gave up his life and reached the highest empire

Tara, his wife, came running and fell over his body, as if about to expire

She began lamenting loudly and cried: “O Vali, you said I am your life

Then, how come you have perished leaving me your adoring wife?”

 

“I thought you always spoke the truth: but you seem to have lied

Had I been your life, like you said, I too must have died!”

Now, Tara turned to Rama in anger, meaning to reprimand Him

Instead, she heaped praise, though her eyes with tears did brim

“O Rama, You are beyond my comprehension

 I cannot make You out, though you stand before me, in person.”

 

“Forget me, a mere monkey! You, the best scriptures cannot pretend to understand 

Not just that, even You cannot fathom Your own self: Your qualities are so grand.

You are not limited by time, space or form

You transcend everything and are beyond all material norm

You came here ostentatiously to fulfill Your father’s word to Your step-mother

Your actions indicate You are the Supreme Person: the exile is just a cover

 

"Kabandha and Viradha, why did You kill?

That was not what Your father wanted You to fulfil!

You sent Jatayu and Shabari to 'Moksha' and terminated myriad demons

And now, You have killed Vali and sent him to the best of regions."

One might think the Lord is far away and hence is incomprehensible 

But Tara proved, even when He is in front of one, He is not understandable.

 

Why did Tara address Rama as "You" which was disrespectful?

To her, none could equal Vali, whom she thought was all-powerful 

In addition, an impolite reference equalled the death punishment 

The angry Tara wanted to fling on Rama that embarrassment 

However, on perceiving Him, her doubts vanished 

And she celebrated Rama, her fury totally vanquished 

 

"You are not readily apparent: you stay hidden in scriptures

When You reveal Yourself to the wise, they go into raptures.

That You have won over Your senses is evident:

I stand here talking, while with Your eyes averted, You stand diffident 

My beauty is so great—everyone gives me a second look

But, I can see that You are dedicated to the single wife You took."

 

You befriended Sugriva and helped him regain his princely status

And the Kingdom You won, You directly gave him, without a fuss."

You are a 'Uttama Purusha'—you are in the category of 'superior persons'

Who undergo miseries to do good to others without rhyme or reasons

Even when You do everything for everyone impartially 

You feel You should have done more and are not satisfied fully."

 

"Your glory is too immense for anyone to reduce

Your qualities are infinite: pure thoughts alone they induce.

Furthermore, here have I been, ranting heatedly for a long while

Yet, You stand with Mother Earth's patience, listening with a smile 

Also, You are clever and show presence of mind

You gave Sugriva a garland to ensure he was easy to find."

 

You answered Vali's questions and mitigated his doubt

His sins and soiled thoughts You were the One to rout

I came here with the express view of denouncing you

But, without saying a word, you have me paying tributes, more than a few!

You have eyes that show radiant red lines all over

With Your loving gaze, mercy and grace, You shower."

 

After acclaiming Rama, Tara wept for Vali, tears copious

Vali's cremation was soon conducted in a manner glorious 

Both Tara and Sugriva, were driven by grief acute

They decided to shed their lives, feeling destitute 

But, Rama and Hanuman pacified the two 

And reminded them: "You have earthly duties to do."

 

Rama instructed Lakshmana thus: "Sugriva's coronation you must conduct

The next few are monsoon months, when the monkeys can recoup and resurrect 

After the rains, let Sugriva send his armies in all directions, as promised

The monkeys shall look for Janaki everywhere: no avenue shall be missed."

Lakshmana went into the Kishkinda kingdom and pronounced Sugriva King

And came back to wait on Rama and to Him, solace, he endeavoured to bring.

 

Rama and Lakshmana went to Mount Prasavana during the rains

And stayed in a cave, Malyavaan, by name, listening to nature's wet strains

Just like the Supreme stays inactive after creation's total dissolution 

Rama too lived in that cave like a lion, subdued, before intense action

And similar to the Lord who creates all the worlds afresh when the time is ripe

Rama awaited the cessation of rainfall, when Ravana He would wipe

 

"The sun's rays impregnate the oceans and clouds are made

After nine months, the heavens deliver them as rain and with it the rivers parade

O Lakshmana, rain is the elixir of life, the nectar that nourishes

If the monsoons fail, due to lack of water, every life form perishes

This scene reminds me of Sita, who, heated by agony, must be shedding tears

My very soul, this rainwater gushing over the heated earth, sears."

 

Lamenting thus, Rama drooped and fell away senseless

Lakshmana gently lifting Him said, "Giving in to despair is useless.

Is it nobler in the mind to suffer the slings and arrows of outrageous fortune? 

Is it wise to lament and wail over events, inopportune?

Or as brave Kshatriyas, should we take arms against our sea of troubles 

And end them? —We must punish the sinners till every eye in their army bubbles.”

 

Let not Your resolution be sicklied over 

With hopelessness: it does not become Your valour.

Using Sugriva's support, that hapless and gentle Sita, we will locate

And through battle, we will make the offenders supplicate."

In this manner, Lakshmana kept Rama's hopes afloat

Once again, Lakshmana's service, Rama gratefully did note.

 

Sugriva, during that period, wallowed in sensuous pleasure

His role as Rama's friend, he forgot, busy revelling in full measure 

But, Hanuman his ever-dutiful and clever minister remained aware

And when the rainy season's end neared, he warned Sugriva to beware

"When you were floundering in distress, Rama came to your aid

Now it's time for your word to be honoured and His help repaid."

 

"Rama is not to be trifled with: His anger is like a forest fire

It burns and annihilates everything without discrimination or fear."

At Hanuman's counsel, Sugriva temporarily regained control of his sense

He ordered: "Within fifteen days, all monkeys must register here their presence."

After putting his commanders in charge of this mission,

Sugriva once again plunged into revelry without intermission.

 

When three months elapsed, the climate became conducive for energetic enterprises

Rama eagerly awaited word from Sugriva, unaware of his tryst with vices

When no message came, Rama's ire was stirred

He said to Lakshmana: "My wrath, Sugriva has incurred."

Lakshmana, nay, Adisesha the hot-tempered serpent queried:

"Shall I kill that betrayer of trust, Sugriva, and have him buried?"

 

Rama, the ever-pacific said: "Friendship have I declared.

I mustn’t break it precipitously: Sugriva's shortcoming must be spared.

I shall give him a chance: you go to him with a warning

And tell him: 'Rama has not closed the route Vali took after dying.'

This should instil in Sugriva a sense of his duty

He will strive to discover Sita, my divine beauty."

 

Lakshmana strode out towards Kishkinda’s gate

At his angry step, monkeys along the way began to prostrate

Standing at the kingdom’s entrance, Lakshmana twanged his bow

Its reverberations shook the earth and sky and white fear did it sow

Sugriva began trembling in sheer terror and looked around with consternation wild

Hanuman swiftly said: “To meet Lakshmana, we must send a female monkey, mild.”

 

“Tara is a clever debater and is best suited to accost Lakshmana first

Her sight and speech would calm that unflinching warrior, I fully trust.”

Thus, it was Tara who boldly went out to meet Lakshmana’s anger:

She knew his fighting code didn’t permit him to put women in danger.

When Lakshmana saw who was approaching, he was disconcerted

Tara’s presence abashed him and he bowed his head, flustered.

 

Tara slowly began to speak and made an attempt to find out 

Why Lakshmana was here, in a mood for a big bout

Lakshmana admonished: “How can Sugriva indulge in ‘Kama’, nay, pleasure-seeking?

He has forgotten his promise to Rama and lies in a stupor without waking!

Tara replied: “I agree ‘Kama’ is wrong. But, what about ‘Krodha’ or anger?

You are displaying that in ample measure now, making us all cower!”

 

“Have you forgotten your teacher Vishwamitra’s weaknesses?

He gave in to ‘Kama’ and ‘Krodha’: for that, there are witnesses!

He lost the fruits of his ‘sandhyavandana’, a brahmin’s three-time worship

He begot a daughter through the divine damsel Menaka’s companionship.”

This retort held Lakshmana and he hurriedly smoothened his frown

And asked to meet Sugriva, after swiftly calming down.

 

Hanuman and Sugriva presently strode forth full of purpose

And relieved Lakshmana’s suspicions, though still a little nervous

Sugriva offered to Lakshmana fruits and roots, choice

Lakshmana declined decidedly, in a clear voice

"I eat only what has first been proffered to Rama," he said

"Anything else I consider as leftover from what a dog is fed."

 

Lakshmana established that a meal presented to the Lord becomes sanctified:

To partake of such food is desirable, as its ill effects get nullified.

Acceding, Sugriva informed: “I have already sent word to all our monkey tribe

We expect them momentarily: their immense powers we cannot sufficiently describe.

Let us go over to Rama and inform Him about the developments in the meanwhile

He can command the army, as though it were his own, in His inimitable style.

 

Sugriva called the male monkeys and asked them to enter into a treaty 

"Let us commit to Rama's service, and reciprocate His kindness per propriety"

The monkeys enthusiastically bid farewell to their families 

And joined the throng to seek Sita whose probity transcended similes   

Now, all the valorous monkeys with Sugriva at their head

Reached Rama's presence and waited patiently to be led.

 

Sugriva stood still and quiet before Rama with folded palms

And put his head upon Rama's feet with his heart full of qualms 

Rama gently lifted Sugriva and held him in a tight embrace

"Probity, prosperity and pleasure, in that order, a king must chase:

Remember, ‘Dharma, Artha and Kama’ are virtues the wise have stated."

Rama advised kindly and asked Sugriva to be seated.

 

Looking at the rapidly surging monkey army, Rama said, feeling satisfied,

"Discuss the impending endeavour with your ministers who stand unified."

Sugriva announced: "Rama's gentle wife Sita the cruel Ravana abducted

The place where she is kept concealed, in haste, must be detected.

I plan to send you all in groups in eight different directions

Look for her in every spot and report back within a month—these are my injunctions."

 

After speaking thus, Sugriva divided his army into eight portions 

And assigning a leader to every one of them, began dispersing instructions 

He described the roads, wilderness, mountains and rivers

As well as caves, lanes, towns, cities, lakes, and other landmarks, diverse

Rama, who was watching the proceedings, was astounded 

At Sugriva's intimate knowledge of locations and his memory unbounded 

 

"Dear Sugriva, how intricately you know this earth!" Rama exclaimed

"Tell me how you acquired this familiarity that deserves to be acclaimed."

Sugriva replied: "When Vali chased me, I ran hither and thither

Looking for a sanctuary, my heart in a perpetual flutter.

Running up mountains, hiding in caves, going underground,

Concealing myself in forests—I just went round and round."

 

"I was forced to remember these locales—for me it was a 'life or death' feat

Until I hid in the Rishyamukha Mountains and found protection at Your lotus feet."

Rama listened carefully and hugging Sugriva said: "Great is your intelligence!

If you observe so much, when running for your life, how much more you'd with diligence?"

Now, Sugriva proceeded to handpick monkeys for the southern direction:

About Sita being taken that way, he did have first-hand information 

 

Sugriva chose Angada to lead the monkeys going Southward

He also asked Hanuman and Jambhavan, the bear, to come forward

Sugriva, turning to Rama, queried: "Do You wish to give any further counsel?

This group, most likely to spot Sita, is waiting to hear Your proposal."

Rama called to Hanuman, after sitting in quiet thought for a while

He removed His finger ring and handed it to him with a smile.

 

"Take this as identification. It holds for Sita and me, memories, most cherished 

That you meet Her in person is a consummation devoutly to be wished."

Hanuman was overwhelmed and humbled at the same moment

The instant Rama's ring touched his hands he recognised his determination, dormant.

Clearly, anyone who received the Supreme's ring would have acquired

Powers enough to see Sita and ascertain Her welfare, as required.

 

When such is the case, why did Rama choose Hanuman for this mission?

Hanuman, being an austere bachelor, failed to understand Rama's passion.

He thought: "Being the embodiment of every kind of power

Why must Rama, so much extremity for love, suffer?"

Having read his mind, Raghava decided: "Hanuman is most suited

To see Sita and understand Her loftiness: this much he has merited."

 

Sugriva warned his monkey army: "Don't step on Venkataadri, the lush mountains

If you do, your sins will be burnt and you will escape this material existence:

To me, it's now important you find Sita; your 'moksha' must wait

For us, embarking on a sacred mission, the 'Seven Hills' are but bait.

And remember: report back within thirty days and impart your news.”

Into Sugriva’s gallant monkeys, his words enthusiasm did infuse.

 

Rama, with hope surging in His heart, thought of Sita's plight

"Let our messengers find you well; may your life not have taken flight!

May your mind remain strong and sane thy brain 

May your heart fill with hope; may no misfortune come between us twain." 

Tears welled and rolled down Rama's chiselled and pale cheeks:

But, He perceived astrological signs that showed His felicity would soon touch peaks.

 

Within the month, the monkey chiefs from all directions except the South returned

Though they had searched diligently, about Sita, no clues they had learned

Now, all their hopes lay pinned on Hanuman and his band 

The delay and anxiety, Rama and His friends, could hardly withstand

However, Sita’s abduction towards the South and Hanuman’s competence

Proved to be factors that gave them all much-needed confidence

 

In the meanwhile, the monkeys moving in the southerly direction

Failed to find success and began to give way to vexation

After combing the Vindhya mountains and its secret hideouts

And after searching even uninhabited spots, the monkeys entertained doubts

“Is Mother Sita even alive? Has Ravana devoured Her?

We cannot return to give negative news: on this we concur.”

 

Soon, Angada fought and killed a demon who appeared on their way:

Upon realising it was not Ravana, the monkeys’ moods once again turned grey

But, Angada motivated them by his sage advice:

“We cannot whine: are we monkeys or mice?

You are right: we cannot face Sugriva without success

His indignation will not spare even one of us.”

 

“However, we do have a little time left and let us not sit still

This much of spirit in us, our elders did instil.

We are enervated by lack of food, water and sleep

But, on Rama’s mission, we cannot act like meek sheep.”

At this, the monkeys grew jubilant and hopeful 

And with refreshed minds, overcame their attitudes slothful.

 

Presently, they found a dark cavity on their patrol

And were intrigued by the secretive and stealthy black hole

Deciding excitedly that this could be Sita’s prison

They ventured into its murky interior to sate their suspicion

Suddenly, they saw a palace beautifully lit and luxurious

The delicious food and drink there made the monkeys delirious

 

While the astonished monkeys hesitated about what to do

A saintly woman walked smilingly into their view

“This is a palace built by a demon; it lies in my care now

If your purpose is pure, you shall go free: this I do vow.”

At this the monkeys explained their mission in detail

And the woman ‘Swayamprabha’ listened raptly to their tale.

 

When she gripped their story, she welcomed Sitarama’s envoys

And plied them with abundant food and drink of choice

Then she transported them using ascetic powers to the Southern-most mountain

And said: "Yonder vast expanse of waters is the southerly ocean.”

Bidding them a benignant farewell, that lady went to her lair

Leaving the monkeys baffled about how to find Sita, the lady fair.

 

On encountering the inexorable ocean, Angada started to panic

“Our time limit has elapsed and about our future prospect, I am frantic.

Presenting ourselves in front of Sugriva without Sita’s bearings is suicide 

Instead, I propose we all fast unto death here: I call upon you to decide.”

Most of those virtuous simians agreed with Angada’s views:

“Let us not return only to disappoint Rama: closed are all our avenues.”

 

But Hanuman criticized Angada for his hasty suggestion

And reminded him about the blame that comes with sedition

He added further: “You are not only intelligent, but also spirited

You know many tactics and with great traits and glory you are credited

Then why speak in this manner of a loser?

Recollect you are the son of Vali admired for his indefatigable valour”

 

Though Hanuman’s words gave the monkeys pause,

They were afraid to face Sugriva and his fearsome laws

The monkeys now began to sit down one by one

While discussing how by their misfortune they were undone

Angada lamented: “Let us follow in the footsteps of Jatayu, that mighty bird

Just like him, let us give up our lives: this decision is not absurd.”

 

“Jatayu fought Ravana and died in battle for Rama’s sake

We too wandered thus far risking our lives without break

Now, having transgressed Sugriva’s deadline and struck a dead-end 

To our pitiable fate, we must our heads bend.”

Sampati, an elderly vulture, who waited nearby expecting a sumptuous meal

Heard Angada’s words and a searing sorrow he began to feel.

 

Hopping over to the monkeys’ side the poor vulture inquired:

“Is my dearest younger brother Jatayu slain? Has he really expired?”

When the monkeys confirmed Jatayu’s murder, Sampati grieved hard

“I saved young Jatayu from the sun: but, my burnt wings, I had to discard.

Alas, what use is that? Before I could, my younger brother has passed on

I let Ravana go without a fight and now I stand woe begone.”

 

"After I lost my wings, I fell on this mountainous region

I decided to give up my life as I had also lost my kingdom and my legion

But, a kind sage named Nishaakara gave me a pair of small wings and courage

And said: "To regain your wings, monkeys you must encourage.

They will come here in the service of Rama looking for Sita, His wife

You must give them Sita's news and then you shall regain your splendid life."

 

Many months ago, I saw Ravana fly over the ocean with a radiant lady

Kidnapping women and persecuting virtuous folk is Ravana’s malady

I may be a helpless creature without my mighty wing

But, I would have challenged Ravana, had I known about Jatayu’s killing.

Let me help you all and also Rama by imparting information:

With Sita, I saw Ravana landing at Lanka, his opulent nation.”

 

All the monkeys felt at these words a surge of relief

That this news was a beacon of hope was their firm belief

Soon they saw Sampati's wings grow in dimension 

And were amazed and grateful for this serendipitous intervention 

Now, they all felt optimistic and with very good reason:

They just had to think of a way to get across the ocean.

 

Angada, Hanuman, and Jambhavan, the elderly bear, were able leaders:

The monkeys had no doubts they would be given the right orders

Sampati had said the distance to Lanka was a hundred 'yojana' over water

Now, Angada became keen to find a monkey who could be Sita's spotter

He himself was capable of jumping a hundred 'yojana' length

But, to search for Janaki and carry back Her message, would he have strength?

 

Angada had to confess his abilities couldn't be trusted to that extent:

Crossing over to Lanka and carrying back news was a dream distant 

Jambhavan said: "Thousands of years ago, the Lord won a Great War 

I circumambulated the earth and proclaimed His victory without par

Now that I am old, I cannot attempt such a feat 

But, there is one amidst us who can do it without getting up heat."

 

"Hanuman here has untold and infinite strength and wit 

By dint of Brahma's word, however, he isn't fully aware of it.

Facing Maruti, Jambhavan said: "You know what you are;

But know not what you may be—trust me, you can go immeasurably far

You are the son of Anjana, a heavenly lady, who a monkey became

Your father is Kesari, but you carry the wind god's lineage too, without blame."

 

"Your arms have the strength of ‘Garuda’, the eagle and the Lord's vehicle

Your speed equals the Wind's; your intelligence and force are too keen to chronicle 

By Brahma's boon, you are indestructible by any missile

And by Indra's, you can command your death—trying to kill you is futile

Concentrate your mind inward and recognise your potential

Your birth is to serve Rama: remember, your part now is essential."

 

Hanuman did as Jambhavan requested—his powers he discerned

And swiftly planned a campaign that his group's approval earned

"At the thought of taking joyful news about Sita to Rama, my heart fills.

I shall assume a huge size and spring into air from the Mahendra Hills.

After landing in Lanka, I shall conduct my search as per the situation 

My mates, pray for good tidings: to our misery, that would be attenuation."

 

Saying thus, Hanuman quickly expanded himself into a hulk 

And began climbing Mt. Mahendra, subjugating it with his mere bulk

The mountain, rich in natural assets, began to spit forth its secrets:

Minerals, animals, meditating sages, trees, flowers and leaflets

When Hanuman pressed down his feet firmly on the mountain's peak

It creaked under the impact, which was an occurrence freak.

 

Hanuman stood, a massive silhouette against the sky

Banging his thighs and shoulders, he let out a whooping cry

And focusing all his energies, preparatory to flying high 

He supplicated to Rama, by summoning Him to his mind's eye

The entire creation blessed the most fortunate monkey at this moment:

Hanuman, Sitarama's messenger and slave, is a remover of every torment
