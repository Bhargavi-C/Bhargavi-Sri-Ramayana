Sundara Kanda

 

Prelude:

Hanuman goes to Lanka in search of Sita by leaping across the ocean 

He spots and gives Her Rama's ring like a life-infusing potion

Sita in turn hands over her ornament and sends Her husband a request:

Only a month would she wait for Rama's arrival and Ravana's conquest

Hanuman advises Ravana to surrender but has his tail set to flame

Unaffected, he returns to Rama and conveying Sita's message, earns fame.

 

The Story in Detail:

 

Hanuman began growing, like an ocean tide on a full moon night

Stopping only when his dimensions were sufficient for his flight

The great simian then shook his hair, and his body he quivered

He roared loudly, and at his dormant power, the world shivered

Hanuman’s long tail coiled and moved, like a sinuous snake:

It had work to do in Lanka—it would set the place ablaze in its wake

 

The 'pavana suta' Hanuman, the wind god's son stood tall

"I shall bring Sita back or Ravana, if I fail to find her," was his call

Then crouching and placing his hands down for stability

The intelligent monkey assumed a pose suited for aerial mobility

Looking at his mountain-like form, the well-wishers of Earth were pleased

"Owing to this Hanuman, from Ravana's grip, the world will be released."

 

Meditating on Rama, Hanuman made a mighty leap and became air-borne

From the force of his jump, mighty trees were uprooted and torn

Flowers dislodging from them scattered and to Hanuman they clung

He resembled a giant cloud cut by lightning: the sight was fit to be sung

Many trees followed his flight like relatives reluctant to separate

The disturbance caused the dwellers of Mt. Mahendra to flee, desperate 

 

The flying Hanuman shone golden like ‘Garuda’, the Lord's carrier

It was apparent to every onlooker that nothing to him could be a barrier

Hanuman’s form in the sky entered and exited clouds and the wind sang loud

His shadow on the ocean's surface looked like a dense cloud

Maruti's eyes twinkled bright like the sun and the moon

He zoomed forth like a meteor anxious to reach the earth soon

 

Hanuman's stretched limbs were like powerful logs cleaving the air

The water beneath sprang up in high tides, a sight rare

The creatures of the sea—crocodiles, whales, and turtles—stood revealed 

‘Samudra’, Ikshvaku's friend, approached ‘Mainaka’ and appealed:

"The wind God helped you, a massive mountain, hide here from Indra's fury

We must help Hanuman, his son, who strives for Rama's glory."

 

The Mainaka Mountain complied with the ocean's request and rose

It intercepted Hanuman and said: "Rest on me; take a refreshing doze

The ocean God and I want to help you, Rama's staunch devotee 

We have kinship with your father and the respected Ikshvaku dynasty."

Hanuman thanked the duo and touched the mountain with respect

He replied: "Until I complete Rama's task, every interruption I must reject."

 

Mainaka and Samudra became delighted at Hanuman's perseverance

They blessed him with good luck and gave him reassurance:

"You will find Sita and return to Rama with Her message

With your intelligence, the couple's sorrows you will assuage."

Hanuman continued his journey with renewed hope and firmness

He was keen to ascertain Sita's welfare and ruminated on Rama with fondness

 

The chiefs of heavenly abodes wanted to gauge Hanuman's capacity—

They enlisted Surasa, the mother of 'nagas', to test Hanuman's sense and tenacity

Surasa, assuming a monster's form, rose in front of Hanuman and challenged:

"Any one who crosses my path must enter my mouth" said she and lunged

Hanuman became bigger but Surasa only widened her mouth further

Now, quickly shrinking, Hanuman entered her mouth and out did he slither

 

Hanuman's wit and presence of mind pleased the heaven's residents

They felt he would get the better of Ravana, though there were no precedents

Anjaneya got their best wishes and continued his flight relentlessly 

Next, he fought ‘Simihika’, the demoness who clutched at shadows, fearlessly 

When she held him back, Hanuman entered her innards and rend her heart:

Thus, he lived up to expectations and overcame every obstacle on his part

 

Soon, Hanuman neared the island of Lanka and spotted its coastline

He saw the place covered in lush green and like a large emerald shine

"I must reduce my size as I land," thought Hanuman with caution

"Before I locate Sita, none should spot me," was his wary notion

Thus, Hanuman assumed his usual size as he came down on a mountain

He made a survey of his surroundings and in his mind organised his intention

 

Lanka proved a veritable paradise with its gloriously planned layout

“No doubt, Lanka seems like heaven on earth!” 

Hanuman thought thus and waited for dark to venture into the city:

He judged it wise to roam Lanka when daylight dimmed in intensity

Nevertheless, when at dusk, he presented himself at the city's gate

He was stopped by the hideous ‘Lankini,’ who protected Lanka's fate.

 

The ogress challenged Hanuman and asked for his identity

Hanuman brought her down with a blow, thus launching Lanka's calamity

Lankini’s eyes started from their spheres and she stared wildly

Her knotted locks parted and each hair stood on end, rigidly 

She looked like a porcupine frightened in its rambles: 

Her hair resembled its quills and lay about in shambles

 

The peerless monkey then began searching far and wide and high and low

But failing to locate Sita, he felt his cheer ebb and his wit refusing to flow

Earlier, he had mistaken for Sita a ravishing woman in Ravana’s private mansion

But had realised his folly and thought:  “Rama’s wife won’t act in this fashion.  

The incomparable Sita must have defied the wicked Ravana's demands

And the helpless lady probably perished at the unscrupulous ogre’s hands.”

 

Hanuman thought along these lines and fell into a deep gloom

How could he return to Rama and announce such awful doom?

Soon, however, he recovered his courage and became optimistic

He decided: "Searching again with a fine-toothed comb would be pragmatic.

I pray to Thee O Rama, may my mission be successful 

O Sita, please reveal Thyself: heed my request, humble and respectful."

 

At once, Hanuman discerned a charming grove full of Asoka trees

Hidden from open view, it lay cloistered in illusory peace

Certain he would see Sita, Hanuman entered this garden 

Almost instantly, from a tree top he spied her, dusty and emaciated with grief’s burden

Hanuman saw in Sita the personification of chaste merit

He thought: “Separated from such a wife, how lives Rama’s spirit?”

 

“In every way, Rama and Sita are made for each other

In beauty, birth, age, virtues, and thought, they match one another.”

When Hanuman marvelled thus, Rama smiled to Himself 

And thought: “I wanted you to see Sita’s speciality yourself

That is why, beloved Hanuman, I chose you to carry my message

My sleepless agitation, you will comprehend and no longer disparage.”

 

About the best way to reveal his form, Hanuman sat contemplating

“I mustn’t jump in front of Sita suddenly: she might fall down swooning.”

While he was pondering thus, there was sudden furore in the place

Sita began trembling and perturbation came upon Her face

As Hanuman watched, a majestic demon with ten heads appeared

“He must be Ravana,” Hanuman thought and behind thick branches disappeared

 

Ravana began to plead with Sita for Her graces and said:

“Be my wife, and upon every type of riches, you shall tread.”

Sita placed between Herself and the demon a blade of grass 

And started to advice him in words that showed Her superlative class

“Turn your mind away from me; I am solely Rama’s property

Place your focus on your wives and children and stick to propriety.”

 

Why did Sita put a blade of grass in front of her before speaking?

Her chastity forbade her from addressing Ravana, a male ill meaning

Also, as a royal lady, she knew the etiquette she must observe

Ravana was a king and in lieu of a seat she offered grass, though with reserve

By placing the grass, between Ravana and herself, Sita created a screen 

She wanted her attitude of formality and distance to be seen

 

Further, it was a warning to Ravana that Rama would mitigate him into grass

Sita also indicated that like worthless grass, Ravana’s riches were crass

Sita moreover pointed out that Ravana was acting like a mindless beast:

That’s why she offered him grass, the food on which bovine creatures feast

Finally, Sita signaled to Ravana that his threat about killing her was in vain

Because, life without Rama was as grass to her, and she would rather be slain.

 

Sita addressed stern words to Ravana and said: “Foul deeds will rise

Though you try to overwhelm them, to good men’s eyes. 

Rama will come to know where I am and who you are

All tongues speak of His valour and yet with Him you wait to spar?

A wise man matches his thoughts and words to his action 

But, though claiming to be brave, you used guile, careless of repercussion.”

 

“Return me to Rama and ask for His pardon, holding His hand in friendship

Be rational and realise, in fear lies your best safety, and escape from hardship.

Remember, my Rama is a He-elephant in rut; you, a mere hare

I do not jest with you: he will crush you and lay Lanka bare.

Is there none to give you good advice and save you from destruction?

Alas, even if there were, you would not listen to their instruction.”

 

On the contemptible and grovelling Ravana, patient and sage advice Sita poured:

She is like a rain cloud, which its goodness can never hoard 

Though Ravana was undeserving to even fall at Rama’s feet for clemency

Sita suggested he hold Rama’s hand, knowing well Her husband’s tendency:

Rama had a generous heart that practised camaraderie on the humblest creature

He would not approve of Ravana falling at His feet, given the ‘rakshasa’s’ stature

 

Sita raised doubts about the presence of wise heads in Ravana’s kingdom

Because, of virtuous folk, his monster-like behaviour gave no symptom

Then she remembered Vibhishana, Ravana’s brother, most righteous,

Who had declared to Ravana that separating Sita from Rama was foolishness

Recollecting his irreproachable character, Sita amended her complaint:

As Ravana’s end was near, his only good counsellor failed to be a restraint

 

Sita struggled to reform Ravana, her senses in turmoil

His maleficent intentions baffled, Ravana’s emotions were in a boil

Hanuman felt wretched: "O that I could intervene and crush the offender!"

Evidently, misery into several lives a single act of sin does render

Finally, Ravana gave Sita two months to change her mind:

“Else, I shall kill you,” he threatened, shameless and unrefined.

 

As soon as he left, Sita shed tears of agony and fear

She concluded: "Death alone can give me succour.

Using my own long and curly hair as rope 

Let me hang myself from a tree: I have lost all hope."

Divining her thought, Hanuman began to panic

And considered: "At this crucial moment, Rama’s name alone would prove a tonic."

 

Sita spoke to Herself: “What should be my fear?

I do not care for life, not when my Rama is not near.

What can death do to my soul?

It is a thing immortal as death’s own toll.

O Sun, your uprise I shall see no more:

Fortune and I part here: my affliction is sore.”

 

Just then, in a sweet, low voice, Hanuman began to sing Rama’s glory

As Sita listened with rapt attention, She forgot her state sorry

Continuing to sing Rama’s name, Hanuman descended from his perch

He stood at Sita’s feet with folded palms, having concluded his search

Taken aback, Sita thought in disbelief and dismay:

“Is this a demon in disguise or is my mind going astray?”

 

By recounting incidents pertaining to Rama, Sita's doubts Hanuman dispelled

"Rama collaborated with Sugriva the monkey King so 'Dharma' can be upheld"

Sita heard Hanuman’s narrative with an incredulous elation

She said: “You have saved my life and Rama’s too, with your devoted oration.

You might be a god, or a monkey, or even a demon: it is no matter

Chanting my Lord’s sweet name, into my parched mind, you’ve splashed nectar.”

 

Hanuman earned the eternal gratitude and praise of the universal mother 

“May smooth success be strewed before your feet: like you, there is no other.”

Sita then asked: “Please describe Rama’s figure and physical attributes

I am eager to know His wellbeing and whether He remembers me in His pursuits.

Hanuman replied: “With my ability for speech, I could describe anything

But, to compare with Rama’s handsome form, there simply is nothing.”

 

“However, I will attempt to tell thee about His physique flawless

His body is well proportioned and majestic, like a tiger reckless

Resembling the full moon are His luminous face and sparkling teeth

One look at his snake-like coils of hair: and creatures forget to breathe 

His nose resembles a creeper; His eyes are its lotus blooms

His red and full mouth is its fruit that in loveliness looms.”

 

“But, all this beauty has waned since You were torn away

Today, He remains listless and to perpetual melancholy, he is prey.

He neither eats nor sleeps nor does He involve in any light-hearted activity

Even if He falls into a doze, He wakes up calling for you, like a bird in captivity

The acute suffering of Rama is there for everyone to see

Nevertheless, He has left no stone unturned in His mission to set you free.”

 

Hearing Hanuman speak, Sita felt happy and sad at the same time

Her heart already full of Rama beat for Him like a mournful chime

That Her husband should be distraught in Her absence made Her smile

The thought of His pangs however brought tears to Her eyes meanwhile

Now, Hanuman retrieved Rama’s ring from his safe keeping

He put it into Sita’s hands and set Her passionately weeping 

 

Sita hugged the ring to Her breast and patted Her eyes with it

She touched it to Her forehead and Her quivering lip she bit

Sita remembered Rama’s hands and shoulders as also His touch

She brought to mind His supple form: His ring became Her crutch 

Sita stood still as though she were embracing Her husband tight

Hanuman looked on with a heaving heart at this touching sight

 

A story known only to Rama, Sita and Lakshmana, Hanuman now narrated

"It seems one day you and Rama swam in the river Godavari and contested.

Lakshmana became the referee and sat upon a boulder

You swam slowly with delicate strokes; Rama was swifter

But, out of love for you, He let you win and lost the competition 

You mocked at Him and laughed aloud, earning Rama's admiration."

 

"Rama recollected this happy incident and asked me to recount it to you

While doing so, He became incensed and roared: 'I shall give the ogres their due.

Let them return Sita to me with her beautiful laughter this very night

Or I shall change the map of this earth, with my bow tip's might.'

Sugriva and Lakshmana pacified Rama and requested Him to simmer down

When I take back news about you, His sorrow Rama will drown."

 

After Hanuman had delivered Rama's message, Sita enquired:

"How did you cross this ocean formidable? Are you not tired?"

Hanuman replied: "I expanded myself and flew across quickly

With Rama's ring on my person, I accomplished this task effortlessly."

At Sita's request Hanuman then displayed his form gigantic 

"You're bigger than 'Varaha', the boar incarnation," admired Sita, ecstatic.

 

"How did Rama befriend Sugriva without my inducing?" Sita queried 

About Rama's benevolence in Her absence, She felt curious and worried

The ‘shastras’ are texts that explain the Lord’s nature 

They state: ‘Lakshmi’s bond with Narayana never does rupture’

Unless the Lord is with His consort, His deeds are unreliable

Compassion and punishment: from Him anything is possible

 

Sita was concerned about the scriptures being reworded:

For creatures to earn God’s leniency, was she no longer needed?

Hanuman appeased her: "Only through you, Rama and we came together

The bundle You dropped had Your blessings and helped us foregather."

Sita heaving a sigh of relief, began recounting a particular incident: 

“Once, while at Ayodhya, I and Rama had a quarrel, a rare event.”

 

“Rama flicked my 'kumkum' the decorative powder for married women

I stopped talking to Him, considering it a silly act and an ill omen.

After a couple of days of ignoring each other, we calmed down

Rama dropped His ring deliberately and asked, "Where’s my ring?" with a frown

I pretended to locate His ring: it had brought us together then

But now, though I have the ring, I wonder if I shall see Him and when.”

 

That loyal monkey thought, "Why must Sita worry here till Rama arrives?

Let me take Her back: that way, pleasure Rama, Sita and everyone derives."

Hanuman said to Sita: "O mother, please climb and sit on my back 

You shall meet Rama in a short while and forget this life black."

Sita refused: "Hanuman, I won't touch another male of my own volition

Hence, I cannot accept your offer on any condition."

 

Hanuman laughed and said: "You are making an excuse flimsy

You are mother to the world; hence, touching me cannot make you uneasy."

At this Sita replied: "But, I shall be scared of the ocean and might fall

And with me on your back, any pursuing demons, how will you stall?"

Hanuman denied these arguments too and contended:

"Please tell the real reason, lady: enough have You pretended."

 

"This universe is Yours and You cannot be scared of an ocean

And being omniscient, that You doubt my ability to fight, is a ludicrous notion."

Sita confessed: "The evil Ravana, I myself could have easily destroyed.

To turn him into ashes, the sheer power of my mind, I could have employed.

But, I do not depend on my capabilities nor look to anyone's hands

My sole support and protection is Rama: I shall wait till here He lands."

 

Hanuman understood: surrendering to the Lord and depending on Him is balm

Trying to help oneself, being against inherent nature, is likely to end in harm

Circumambulating Sita and bowing to Her feet, Hanuman prepared to leave

"Please give a token of identification for Rama—you need not grieve."

 Sita gave Hanuman a jewel from Her hair and narrated another story

"This incident known only to me and Rama, like ever, speaks His glory." 

 

"Once, at Chitrakoota, Rama rested with His head on my lap

I sat still, fanning Him and gazing happily on His handsome map.

Suddenly, a demonic crow swooped down and came at me with lust

I tried to shoo it, but blood started dripping as it pecked at my breast 

Rama woke up and charged a blade of grass with the power of a weapon

He released it and let it go after the crow, who was a disguised demon

 

"The crow flew all over the world, requesting protection 

And finally went to Lord Brahma himself, seeking to avoid destruction 

Brahma advised him: "Ask for pardon at Rama's feet, the ultimate resting place."

The crow fell at Rama's feet and placed on Him its claws instead of its face

I turned its face to Rama's feet and requested: "Forgive the misled creature."

Rama's weapon poked its eye, but spared its life—testimony to Rama's nature."

 

"One more incident let me recount for Rama's pleasure

At Chitrakoota lived a parrot who amused us when we were at leisure

We wanted to give it a name and all three of us pondered

Rama said, 'Let us name it Kaikeyi, our mother must be honoured.'

This is my husband's tender heart that knows no animosity 

In my Rama's nature, there is nothing but high-minded generosity."

 

"May fortune be yours, Hanuman! Give Rama my Jewel and my stories

Tell Him I shall not live beyond a month surviving these demons' furies

I worry how Rama and Lakshmana will reach Lanka by crossing the sea 

You must place them on your shoulders and bring them over with glee."

Hanuman replied: "With Rama leading us, our monkey army can cross any ocean 

Just await your liberation: to remain anxious now, there is no reason!"

 

“Till Rama comes, I must cling on to life,” spoke Sita to Her mind

Hanuman, meanwhile considered: "Ravana's might, I must find.

The best way to do it would be by creating a ruckus 

Let me begin by destroying this garden and making a fuss."

Hanuman assumed a big size and started uprooting trees

The demonesses and demons guarding the place, he began to seize.

 

The scared fiends ran to their chiefs and reported the rampage

The demon leaders came to investigate and witnessed Hanuman's rage

Rama's messenger killed many, among them Akhsha Kumara, Ravana's son

To combat Hanuman, then came Indrajit, Ravana's favourite scion

Indrajit released 'Brahmastra' the weapon having Lord Brahma's force

Pretending capitulation stood that monkey of sagacious resource.

 

Maruti wanted to meet Ravana quickly and reveal Rama's intention

He would warn him about Rama's impending intervention

He allowed himself to be dragged in ropes to Ravana's Royal assembly 

When he saw the unprincipled King, he could barely control his fury

Ravana commanded Hanuman to speak and explain his offence

Hanuman announced: "I'm Srirama's messenger; into you, I'll knock some sense."

 

"I am Hanuman, the minister of Sugriva, Vali's brother

For his wrongdoing, Rama dispatched Vali to realms farther."

When Ravana heard this information, his body jolted in shock

"Has Vali really been killed? Or me, do you dare mock?

Hanuman replied: "With a single arrow of Rama's, Vali was felled

Remind yourself: you lost to Vali once; by false complacence, don't be lulled."

 

"You can retain your life and save from obliteration, your kith and kin

Return Sita with reverence to Rama and repent for your sin."

Ravana laughed with derision; his mind blocked by lust

He said: "To a puny human's whims, my actions I refuse to adjust.

Here, kill this insolent monkey right at this spot

Let his mission to report Sita's whereabouts come to naught."

 

Vibhishana, Ravana's younger brother, was steeped in justice

He cried: “O brother, killing an envoy is unfair: you will land in a crisis

Let him go back to the princes and give an account of your strength

And explain the difficulty of reaching Lanka, crossing the ocean’s length.”

Ravana agreed with Vibhishana and Hanuman’s life he decided to spare 

He however commanded: “Set the ape’s tail on fire–it will give him a good scare.”

 

But, when Hanuman’s tail was set ablaze, he climbed high on to a roof

And setting the entire city on fire, of his prowess he gave proof

Suddenly, Hanuman recalled Sita and worried about Her fate 

He rushed to the Asoka garden, bitterly regretting his haste

However, Sita was well and said: "I've commanded Fire not to burn you

You will be Rama's servitor forever and remain under His benevolent view."

 

Hanuman touched Sita's feet with reverence and bade farewell

He was eager to meet his group and his sweet news tell

Rushing to the mountain near the ocean coast, Hanuman grew 

In no time, he reached the ocean's other shore and sighted his anxious crew

The monkeys had been waiting for Hanuman's arrival without food and water

Seeing Hanuman's approach, they thrilled and began to chatter

 

Jambhavan detected success from Hanuman's jubilant attitude 

He cried, "Hanuman has surely seen Sita safe; we owe him our gratitude."

Hanuman landed amidst his clamorous comrades with a gleeful yell

The monkeys began to jump in delight when he announced, "All is well."

The group wasted no time in heading back to where Rama was

On their way, 'Madhuvana', Sugriva's fruit garden did they pass

 

The hungry simians decided to sate their hunger and thirst 

Into that precious and favourite garden of Sugriva’s, they burst

Ransacking the trees and plucking the fruits, the monkeys made merry

To stop their frenzy, 'Dadimukha', the caretaker of the garden did hurry

But, the monkeys failed to listen to the warnings of Sugriva's uncle

So, he hurried to Sugriva and said, "Your boisterous army, I cannot tackle."

 

"They arrived a while ago and are destroying your favourite garden of fruits

My appeals went unheeded: I failed to control the fermenting brutes."

On hearing Dadimukha's account, Sugriva began leaping 

He ran to a rock and upon it, his tail he started beating

A bemused Rama exclaimed: "Your favourite garden is being wrecked

And here you are running amok with happiness unchecked!"

 

Sugriva came to Rama with a beaming face and said: 

"Sita is well—that's why our army is celebrating without dread

They know anyone who dares disobey my orders gets the noose

They are well past their time limit—and yet they run loose

That means Sita's welfare is ascertained 

Understandably, their joy and pride they have not contained."

 

Just then Jambhavan, Hanuman, Angada and the others arrived

Upon perceiving their joyful expressions, a modicum of relief Rama derived

One by one the monkeys who went in search of Sita came up to Rama 

They thumped His back in excitement and said: “Found Sita,” 

Those present there felt, “It’s a good thing, at ‘Madhuvana’ the monkeys stopped

Else, even more forceful slaps, on Rama's back they would have dropped!

 

Rama watched contentedly as all the monkey folk competed

To tell the news of Sita and see His face light up, elated

Finally, Hanuman came near Rama and said: “I saw Sita face to face

She sat like the statue of chastity, and of cheer, she had no trace.

She was soiled and worn, and expects your arrival every moment

Her jewel and an imploring message, through me she sent.

 

Hanuman then handed Rama the jewel from Sita’s hair

On seeing it, Rama swooned, stricken by thoughts of His mate fair

Reviving soon, He asked Hanuman to describe Sita’s condition:

“How is Sita bearing up under fate’s cruel assault? I await your rendition”

Hanuman said: “Sita is holding on to life with Your thoughts alone

She's surrounded by frightening fiends, who for their cruelty, are well known.”

 

Then Hanuman narrated the incidents just as ‘Mythili’ had instructed

Rama became convinced of the wellbeing of His wife who had been abducted  

Hanuman went on to convey Sita’s request to Rama and His troops

"She desires You to reach Lanka and destroy Ravana and his vile groups."

As I left, I said: “Be cheered, great is the prowess of the monkeys and bears

Do not panic: Rama will lead us here, have no mares.”

 

 

************ Thus ends Sundara Kanda **************

Sriramajayam

Jai Hanuman

Asmad Gurubhyo Namaha
