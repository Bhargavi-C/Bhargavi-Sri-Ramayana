Yuddha Kanda

 

Prelude:

Rama and His troops march south and reach the ocean's shore 

Vibhishana surrenders to Rama unconditionally, quitting Ravana's door

The monkeys build a bridge across the ocean with its deity's cooperation 

Rama marches to Lanka and vanquishes Ravana, ending evil's incarnation 

Sita and Rama unite and return to Ayodhya with Vibhishana, Sugriva, and friends

To perpetuate universal peace and felicity, Ayodhya’s throne, Rama ascends.

 

The Story in Detail:

 

Rama, listening to Hanuman's account of Sita, gazed upon him with gratitude

He heartily embraced and thanked the monkey, looked on by the multitude:

When Hanuman went as Rama’s messenger taking His lotus-eyed blessing

All he got was fire to his tail—an event rather distressing

But, when he came as Sita’s messenger, he got Rama’s embrace:

Because Her red-lotus-like eyes always shower compassionate grace.

 

Sita’s adored husband then, delayed not an instant 

He led Sugriva and His army for the ocean, distant

Lakshmana, Jambhavan, Hanuman, Nala, Nila and other Chiefs 

Along with scores of monkeys and bears set out to punish Lanka's thieves 

Within twelve days, Rama’s flood-like troops reached the Southern tip

Now Rama pondered: “We must reach Lanka: how to make that trip?”

 

On the other side meanwhile, Ravana prepared for war

He worried: “Rama’s arrival here, can the ocean mar?”

His sanguine courtiers adulated him and denied every danger 

But, wise Vibhishana gave advice: “Your life, do not endanger.

Rama is not an ordinary mortal as you think

He is Sriman Narayana, in whom everything does sink.”

 

“You have already committed a great sin by snatching Sita away by stealth

Do as Hanuman suggested: return Her and retain your glory and wealth

Since the day you brought Rama’s virtuous wife into our terrain 

There have been bad omens everyday: their message is plain.

Do not listen to the counsel your foolish courtiers and relatives give

Listen to your conscience and reach Rama: He will forgive.”

 

Repent what is past and avoid what is to come

Repair your actions and save Lanka from a fate gruesome 

If you do not return Sita, a battle is imminent

You will fall to earth and mingle in dust: Rama shall be dominant

Do not spread compost on sinful weeds

You will only make them ranker with your ill deeds."

 

With Vibhishana’s advice, Ravana turned furious

He fumed: “I have nurtured in my bosom a snake injurious

Instead of supporting me and allaying my fears

You utter words that are like hot venom to my ears.”

Ravana’s son Indrajit too joined in his father’s tirades:

“This uncle of mine always criticises our exploits and crusades.”

 

On Vibhishana, Ravana and his cronies began to heap insults:

They failed to distinguish right from wrong, those retarded adults

“After eating Ravana’s salt and being his flesh and blood

You dare speak against him and on his actions throw mud?”

Challenged thus by Ravana's unwise kith and kin

Vibhishana decided it was time to distance himself from sin.

 

Ravana, could not digest Vibhishana’s wise counsel

His ears were sealed against just words because he was a scoundrel

Even if he didn’t relish righteous advice, he could have imbibed it

Simply because it was from his own brother with an upright spirit

But, ‘Kaalaa’ or the God of Death had positioned himself in Ravana’s ear:

That is why even a single sane word, he failed to hear.

 

Vibhishana decided where his loyalty must lie in this situation 

'Right always comes before relationship' was Vibhishana's contention

He bowed low to his elder brother and tearfully pledged:

"Upon me, disloyalty you and the others have alleged.

Though I did all I can to show you the 'Dharmic' track

Your fate has made you cling to your destructive tack."

 

”Alas, I cannot save you from sin; you are destined for pain

My life here ceases—I must quickly reach ‘Dharma's’ domain

I bid you farewell and liberate myself from here, this very moment

My wife, children and family, I give up without further comment

From now, my all is Rama, who is every soul's ultimate sustenance 

I leave Lanka with a light heart, without repentance."

 

Thus said Vibhishana, the perspicacious discerner of truth 

And comporting his mind, came over to Rama, the epitome of ruth 

Vibhishana flew holding his mace in a grip steady and powerful 

With him he took four of his followers, true and faithful

The wise rakshasa now reached Rama's place with folded palms:

Was he glad to be in a camp that listened to younger brothers without qualms!

 

Every soul is the ‘paramatma’s’ property that none can poach

Vibhishana's mace represents the soul that submits to the Lord’s approach

Vibhishana, coming to Rama, became ‘Shrimaan’ in the revered Valmiki’s mind:

Because, he realised his spiritual destiny and himself to the Supreme, he did bind

Thus, Vibhishana became the possessor of wealth of every sort

And every one of his materialistic snares, he easily did thwart.

 

The essence of Sri Ramayana is contained in ‘Sharanagati’—that is, surrender

Embodying all its components, ‘Vibhishana Sharanagati’ stands alone in splendour

There are three standard paths that lead to release from the birth cycle:

‘Karma yoga,’ ‘Gyana yoga’ and ‘Bhakthi yoga’ yield results, more than a trifle

‘Karma yoga’ needs knowledge of every scripture 

‘Gyana yoga’ entails thinking incessantly of God’s nature

 

‘Bhakthi yoga’ is love for God mingled with constant thought:

All three means with difficulties are fraught

One needs for ‘Karma yoga’ and ‘Gyana yoga’ thousands of years 

Then, when sins are washed away, the route for ‘Bhakthi yoga’ clears

Obviously, the three paths call for immense determination and sense control: 

These therefore are difficult to follow for the common soul 

 

One finds a house owner attending to his property and caring for it

If it is damaged or usurped by someone, the owner is badly hit.

Every creation is owned by the 'Paramatma' or the Supreme:

Therefore, He worries about them in the extreme

When His creations fail to reach Him, Narayana, the universal owner, suffers a sense of loss

To help His children reach Him easily, He paved the ‘Sharanagati’ path, in His mercy without cause

 

The first three yogic paths are ‘Saadhyopaya’ that involve one’s own efforts

‘Sharanagati’ is ‘Siddhopaya’ a ready-made way with the best of comforts

Surrender has neither rule nor stipulation 

It simply calls for total submission

Anyone can perform ‘Sharanagati’ for any fruits at anytime from anywhere

The only condition is: one must direct it towards Narayana, the eternally aware

 

For every person who is convinced about his own inability to reach salvation

Surrender, with its six components, becomes the simplest solution:

‘Sharanagati’ involves complete faith that the Lord is the only recourse

And the prayer that, to break mortal coils, He must be the force

It means doing what He has ordained and refraining from those He has not

It requires affirming one’s inability to give anything but the soul without a blot.

 

An analogy best describes the Sharanagati dictum:

A baby cat carried by its mother shows perfect wisdom

Though it seems the mother's jaw might crush the fragile kitten

She carries it with care and ensures the baby is not bitten

All the baby does is give itself up to its mother with credence:

Thus must every soul surrender to the super soul without reluctance.

 

A baby monkey, on the other hand, takes the onus for its own transport

As the mother moves, it clings to her tummy for support

If the baby’s grip falters, down it falls and ends:

Attempting to reach the Lord by one’s own efforts, towards misadventure, tends

In ‘Sharanagati’, one must adopt the clever kitten’s attitude:

The Creator Himself leads the surrendered soul to His feet with rectitude

 

A reservoir or tank is another example to clarify the ‘Sharanagati’ principle

One digs it to save water during rains, which are unpredictable

Akin to a tank is one’s announcement of ‘Sharanagati’ or readiness for salvation

The ‘Paramatma’ bestows His rain-like mercy as per His inclination

One cannot dictate when the rain must pour

But, if one fails to build a reservoir, rainwater, he cannot store.

 

An additional simile is a man who wants to sit atop an elephant and has two options

The elephant kneeling and lifting him up or the person using his own contraptions

Of the two, the former is the better method, easy and free from risk

But, the person must remain still, when the elephant stoops to whisk

If the man doubts the pachyderm’s intention and tries to act

He is bound to be dashed to the ground and cannot remain intact

 

A ‘Sharanagata’ is a person who is desirous of surrender

He must have complete faith in the Lord: over this, there can be no blunder 

A child who gets beaten by his mother for mischief

Does not run away to another’s mother even for a time brief

In the same manner, a person who has surrendered to God

Must remain complacent and staunch that His mercy is never flawed

 

When a soul surrenders, he realizes that he has nothing to barter 

He shows that to the Lord by ‘Anjali Mudra’, that is, bringing his palms together 

By putting his feet together and prostrating to the Supreme 

The surrendering person indicates he stays put under His regime

Vibhishana’s four followers represented his arms and legs

Joining them, he had reached Rama, despite having lived among the dregs. 

 

Vibhishana came to Rama shedding every earthly attachment

He sought Rama's feet as the sole mode for spiritual advancement

The rakshasa acted in a manner that aligned with the Lord’s will

And refused to do things that would fit his injunctions ill

Vibhishana held his palms together, indicating he had nothing to present

His soul he offered devoutly, with his wise head bent

 

From Lanka, Vibhishana flew into the air, that rakshasa marked for eternal fame

'And to the location where Rama stayed with Lakshmana, he quickly came':

Every one of these words has distinct significance for ‘Jivatma’ in the universe

The ‘place where Rama is’ removes sin and has sanctity diverse

By ‘enlisting a devotee like Lakshmana’ one earns the Lord’s grace easily

A devotee understands another’s travails and in convincing the Lord, helps readily

 

A person’s movements are typically explained relative to his home

“He has ‘gone’ out” is what describes a person out to roam

When he returns home, “He has ‘come’” gives his position

Thus, “Vibhishana” ‘went’ to Rama must have been Valmiki’s narration

But, the high-souled sage sang ‘quickly came to Rama’:

Because Rama is everything, including home, for Vibhishana—and every ‘Jivatma’

 

Spotting Vibhishana from afar, the monkeys became agitated

They began throwing boulders and trees at him, feeling aggravated

Vibhishana held his powerful mace between his folded hands

The monkeys said: “We must destroy that flying ogre, before he lands.

From the way he wields his mace, this demon’s fighting skills are obvious

He excels in the usage of many weapons: about this, none can be dubious.”

 

But, Vibhishana’s face was tranquil and showed humility and surrender

The monkeys could perceive his expression, gentle and tender

Without climbing down from the sky, that ‘rakshasa’ prayed:

“Tell Rama, Vibhishana is here—let the information not be delayed.

I have renounced my brother Ravana, the kingdom of Lanka and my family

I have come here, seeking deliverance at Rama’s lotus feet, gladly.”

 

Sugriva said: “You must wait, Rama is busy and cannot be disturbed.”

But, Vibhishana pleaded: “Go at once, before my mind becomes perturbed.”

The monkeys went to Rama and delivered Vibhishana’s request

Sugriva cried: “You must kill the rakshasa; he is likely a wicked pest.”

All the elders from the monkey and bear troops agreed with Sugriva’s verdict

“This is a wrong time and place to surrender: Vibhishana You must evict.”

 

When Sugriva asked for Vibhishana’s execution, Rama’s face turned crimson

Recognising disapproval, the others monkeys sought only Vibhishana’s expulsion:

Though everyone felt that the ogre must be slain without scruples

They did not want to hurt Rama who had steadfast principles

Hence, they modified their opinions and softened their demand

And concluded, “We have said enough Rama; we now await Your command”  

 

Hanuman had not spoken a word while the others deliberated

Rama said: “Speak Hanuman, why is your opinion unstated?”

That wise monkey now replied: “Vibhishana was born under a charitable star. 

Though a rakshasa, the evil nature of his tribe, does not him mar.

When I was at Lanka, he spoke against his brother with courage and conviction

Clearly, he has a sense of right, and he states it without inhibition.”

 

“He must be granted a place at Your lotus feet 

I recommend You make him Lanka's King after Ravana’s defeat.”

Rama heard the opinion of every monkey with quiet patience:

He listened to their reasoning without annoyance

‘Give everyone your ear and few your voice’: #

By this dictum lived the wise Rama, by choice.

 

All the monkeys, including Hanuman, looked at Rama expectantly

His words always sounded like nectar, spoken benignantly 

A smiling Rama said, “I shall now state my decision

I take a half each from Sugriva’s and Hanuman’s suggestion:

I will accept Sugriva’s opinion that Vibhishana has designs fake

And, Hanuman’s suggestion that he should be included, I will take.”

 

“The fundamental principle of the Ikshvaku race is to bestow protection

On those who are weak and those who seek it: whatever their station

I will protect Vibhishana, even if he has intentions hostile

If I act otherwise, my dynasty and ancestors, I shall defile

Whatever might be Vibhishana’s plans and whatever his desire

I take him on my side and give him shelter, without ire.”

 

Rama has given His promise of shelter to one and all who reach Him

More: to one who offers no opposition, He pours His grace to the brim

All one must understand is: the Lord awaits His child's submission

He is then only too happy to show His mercy without repression:

‘Sriman Narayana’ goes by many a different name

Each represents a quality and reflects His eternal fame

 

Once 'Parvati' asked 'Shiva': "Which of the Lord's names is best?

Which name is equal to His thousand names—uttering which name one is blest?"

Shiva replied: "Srirama's name that appeals to every fortunate heart

Is practically equal to the 'Sahasranama': this fact you cannot distort

By chanting with devotion the thousand names of the Lord

One returns to Vaikunta, easily breaking his material cord.

 

When Rama returned to Vaikunta, He took every creature back

From the meanest grass and ant to the largest animals and humans, He did pack.

Anyone wanting eternal freedom from the birth-death cycle

Must chant 'Srirama, Rama, Rama', which is the material bonds' sickle

Because Rama's name yields the same result as the Sahasranama chanting

Shiva, that epitome of wisdom, declared as supreme Rama's name, enchanting.

 

Rama now turned to Sugriva and said gently: “My friend, worry not.

Towards me, none can dare hold a harmful thought

Just the tip of my little finger is enough to demolish any band

For that, I do not need My bow or arm or My hand

Go and bring Vibhishana to me right this moment

And even if Ravana has come, get him too—have faith in my powers dormant.”

 

“If Vibhishana is here, it means life for a handful of ogres

But if Ravana has come, peace for entire communities, it augurs.” 

Rama specially sent Sugriva to fetch Vibhishana to His presence:

He believed in building trust and wiping away resistance

Not just that, when one approaches the Lord with a devotee at the head

He feels more ready to grant sanctum without question and moves ahead

 

Sugriva went at once and brought Vibhishana for Rama's audience

Gladly came forward that rakshasa, untainted by nescience.

He prostrated at Rama’s feet, while from his eyes tears flowed freely

Rama lifted his pure-souled devotee and hugged him compassionately

He said: “In Guha and Sugriva, I gained two brothers honourable

With you, we have become seven sons to my father Dasaratha, venerable.” 

 

The Omniscient One then declared, “I make you King of Lanka, rest assured.”

Vibhishana replied, “I came here seeking only thy feet: the feet Sugriva secured.”

Rama beckoned to Lakshmana and asked him to fetch water from the ocean

He coronated Vibhishana then and there, without further commotion

Rama then proclaimed, “Ravana with his wicked clan shall perish

I shall eliminate, from the horizon of ‘Dharma,’ that blemish.”

 

Shurpanaka sought the Lord without His consort

Consequently, her nose and ears, she lost

Ravana hankered for Sri sans Her Lord

He lost his life for this thought, haphazard

Vibhishana came to the Lord having venerated Sri before:

He received the crown of ‘Rama Samrajya’, therefore

 

Presently, Rama and His associates deliberated about reaching Lanka’s door:

“What would be the best mode to cross to the ocean’s other shore?”

 Vibhishana spoke up softly and respectfully when thus addressed:

“For three days, you must pray to the Ocean God, undeterred.”

Vibhishana suggested ‘Sharanagati,’ because for him it had worked

In his mind, the question “Would it work for the Lord of lords?” never lurked.

 

Rama lay meditating on the bare ground, His lotus face turned East

With His snake-like arms curled up as a pillow, he resembled a resting beast

In the meanwhile, Ravana sent to Sugriva, Shuka, his emissary

Ravana’s offer of friendship, that ogre conveyed in a manner wary

A furious Sugriva, rejecting the offer with disgust, sent a fitting reply 

“I shall fight you from the side of Rama, with whom none in any world can vie.”

 

Rama continued His prayer to the divine being of the oceans

Desiring a passage to Lanka, he lay controlling His emotions

However, even after three days and nights, the ocean deity refused to appear

Rama, black with anger, struck a pose that inspired awful fear

He commanded Lakshmana to bring ‘Sarnga’, His divine bow 

Lakshmana complied, but got the weapon without any arrow.

 

Rama twanged His bow and exclaimed: “Lakshmana, I need an arrow!”

Lakshmana went slowly and brought an insignificant specimen, narrow.

Rama said: “For defeating the powerful ocean how will such an arrow do?

Get me a bigger one, dear brother, what is with you?”

A reluctant Lakshmana brought an arrow slightly better than the previous one:

It still wasn’t what Rama had in mind and so He chided: “What have you done?”

 

Why was Lakshmana behaving in this strange manner?

Wasn’t He the ‘avatar’ of the thousand-hooded snake, impossible to shatter?

In usual practice, Rama had only to turn and look at Him:

When Lakshmana would jump up and ask: “Should I kill? Is that Your whim?”

But, this occasion was different: the ocean was Lakshmana’s kin

He too was the Lord’s bed and thus to ‘Adisesha’ was akin.

 

Both Lakshmana and the Ocean were the Lord’s workers

One wanted to save the other, showing solidarity amongst servitors

Finally, Lakshmana fetched an arrow suitable

When Rama took aim, the ocean god appeared, his fright palpable.

Humbly joining his palms, that divine being supplicated

“O Rama, pardon me,” he cried, scared of being mutilated.

 

The moment the Ocean bowed, Rama's pique evaporated 

He said: "Do not be afraid. With My arrow, your enemies shall be frustrated."

Rama is ready to modify His decision when His opponent capitulates:

The Supreme alone shows the nobility such ready forgiving stipulates

The ocean god then pointed to a place where perpetrators of evil lived

Rama's sure arrow found its mark and the skulking wickedness it killed.

 

‘Sagara,’ the ocean God, said: "Your ancestors did me a good turn.

By serving You, my gratitude I must return

I shall have my waters calm: let the monkeys build a bridge 

By directing them, ‘Nala’ and ‘Nila’ can, the time for completion abridge

Let the monkeys write Your glorious name on rocks

I shall keep them all afloat like so many wooden blocks."

 

Sugriva now commanded the engineer monkeys ‘Nala-Nila’ to begin 

Every monkey and bear, happy to play a part in Sitarama's service, pitched in

Breaking down mountains and carrying large boulders 

They resembled hills carrying hills on their shoulders

The monkeys and bears wanted to complete the bridge quickly

"Well before His next mealtime, Rama must reach Lanka” they thought lovingly.

 

A large group of squirrels now joined this labouring throng

Wanting to serve Rama physically, they had come along

The squirrels declared: “O monkeys, about building a bridge you have no notion.

Not gluing the rocks together, you will soon set them in motion.”

Saying thus, they went and immersed their furry forms in the ocean’s water

Then, gathering sand on their bodies to shake between the rocks, they did totter 

 

The squirrels proclaimed: “We, who do the plastering, are the supervisors

You must listen to our directions: we are entitled to be advisors.”

Chaffing and motivating one another thus, those fortunate creatures toiled

While Rama, the universal parent, looked on with delight unspoiled

The long bridge was completed in a matter of five days 

Rama’s army got ready to cross over to Lanka without further delays

 

Shuka and a spy Sharana meanwhile delineated Rama’s indestructible might

They also carried versions of the simians’ infinite prowess and will to fight

After updating Ravana, they advised him to give up the idea of battle

“Even now Rama will forgive you—He is made of tolerant mettle.”

But, Ravana’s mind refused to accept that Rama was the Supreme incarnate:

Who can stop a moth that goes into fire to meet its wretched fate?

 

Next, Ravana sent another spy to find the status of Rama’s progress

He reported they had walked on a bridge, making into Lanka a quick ingress

Ravana, daunted, gathered information about Rama’s troops

He felt agitated on learning about the hordes of monkeys arrayed in groups 

As usual, Ravana decided to stoop to deception and trickery

He approached an expert conjurer on his side, steeped in treachery

 

Ravana instructed the demon-magician to conjure a head:

“It must resemble Rama’s and produce in Sita horror and dread

Also, make a large bow and arrows just like the ones Rama owns

They must be realistic and must freeze Sita’s bones.”

The magician quickly did as he was asked 

Ravana, seeking Sita’s submission, into Asoka grove passed

 

“Rama was killed in battle; His associates were conquered.

You had better fulfil my desire, because your only hope is shattered.

I have brought the slain Rama’s bloody head and His bow 

Also look at His arrows, which for evidence I proceed to show.” 

Ravana stood in front of Sita and displayed Rama’s head

Poor Sita swooned seeing the severed head of the Lord she had wed

 

When She regained consciousness, Sita began lamenting piteously

Considering Kaushalya’s predicament and her own fate, she cried repeatedly

Then Sita begged Ravana thus: “You killed my only support, now kill me too.

I want to join my husband in the upper realms to where He flew.”

Just then an emissary came to Ravana in a hurry

On getting his message that Rakshasa plunged into worry.

 

Ravana left quickly, and Sita continued her tearful mourning undiminished

But, as soon as Ravana went, Rama’s head and bow too vanished

Sita, taken aback, began to wonder about the genuineness of what she had seen

Just then, a kind ogress, and an attendant, began reassuring Rama’s queen

“Rama is arrived at Lanka with His brother and an army, powerful and sprawling

Ravana resorted to mean duplicity, because this battle he is scared of losing.”

 

“Rest assured, Rama is well; the good portents around you, please observe.

Your husband will destroy Ravana and rescue you: believe this without reserve.”

At that instant, Sita saw and felt many auspicious omens

She realised Rama was unharmed and would defeat the demons

Offering prayers for her husband’s well-being and victory

Sita sat down composed and began her wait for liberty

 

The sounds of kettledrums and conches from Rama’s army induced panic

Ravana’s bosom began to tremble and his angry passions became volcanic

The king of Lanka called his council of ministers to chart further action

Malyavan, his maternal grandfather, advised: “Seek peace and evade desolation.

I see the most hideous omens for our tribe; total destruction they portend

Make truce with Rama by restoring His wife to Him and your iniquity, amend.”

 

Ravana ignored the old ogre’s words and began to draw his plan

He stationed powerful army heads at Lanka’s gates to safeguard his clan 

Vibhishana sent his companions as spies to learn the enemy’s arrangements

They came back with information about Ravana’s defence placements

Rama sent monkey chiefs like Angada and Hanuman to storm Lanka’s gates

He decided to remain on Mt. Suvela debating tactics with other associates

 

Soon, Rama, Lakshmana, Vibhishana and Sugriva reached the mountain's crest

With a view to understanding Lanka’s layout before taking a rest

They spotted Ravana’s magnificent mansion with its numerous pillars

And also many towers, waterways, buildings and gardens filled with colours

Sugriva then saw the ten-headed Ravana surveying the scene from a tall tower 

In red-eyed anger, he leapt close to the demon and startled him with his power 

 

Sugriva proceeded to snatch Ravana’s crown and dashed it to the ground

Then he and Ravana, wrestling one another, began to go round and round

After many difficult maneuvers and hard punching, the two became tired

Ravana contemplated resorting to magic, when Sugriva quickly retired

The vexed and astonished demon looked thoroughly baffled

And the satisfied Sugriva returned to his camp unruffled

 

The monkey army were highly delighted by Sugriva's courage

They honoured him by going on a typical ape-like rampage

But a worried Rama hugging Sugriva cried, “How could you take such a chance?

Promise me you will do nothing foolhardy: you must at once take that stance.

What would be the point of Sita’s release

were I to lose you? My dear friend, then my life too would cease!”

 

After being duly pacified, Rama ordered Lakshmana: “Let our army remain alert.

Divide them into battle arrays so that any sudden charge we can readily avert.

The unruly winds, glowering clouds, and the trembling earth forebode calamity

Many intrepid warriors from both sides will die, I can say with certainty."

Soon, Rama, who trod virtue’s path, climbed down from the mountain peak

And at the appropriate moment, He marched forth with His army, havoc to wreak.

 

Upon reaching the gates of Lanka, Rama decided to send one last message of peace

He called Angada and said: “Tell Ravana his atrocities he must cease.

If he is willing to return Sita unharmed, we will return without a battle

Else, there would destruction that the three worlds would rattle.”

Carrying Rama’s message to Ravana, Angada boldly made his voice heard

“Seize and crush that arrogant monkey,” a furious Ravana ordered

 

But, Tara and Vali’s son escaped to the palace roof with a powerful leap

He cried mockingly: “Ravana, in the battle, the fruits of your sins, you shall reap.”

Then that valiant young ape crushed the top of Ravana’s mansion into tiny bits

Ravana felt fear gripping his mind and his quivering heart going into fits.

The demons seeing the ocean of monkeys at their gates became fretful

Their king too was uneasy at Rama’s unfailing progress and felt far from restful

 

Angada returned to Rama and conveyed Ravana’s refusal

Rama realised the demons would not escape their punishment brutal

He commanded His monkey troops to commence fighting 

The monkeys blew conches and beat drums to announce their coming

They broke Lanka’s trees and mountaintops to use as weapons

Their nails and teeth too became tools to teach battle lessons

 

Many of the monkey chiefs fought ferocious demons

For their armies, their brave deeds became motivating beacons

Angada fought Indrajit, and Lakshmana held Virupaksha 

While Rama’s relentless arrows struck ‘rakshasa’ after ‘rakshasa’

Jambhavan, Sugriva, and Hanuman planted terror among the enemy lines

Fierce fighting continued, and the intrepid warriors knew no confines

 

The demons became more active and rejuvenated after sundown:

Though the battle carried on, Rama’s army fought without breaking down

Rama hunted tirelessly, and Lakshmana proved his immeasurable prowess 

Angada destroyed Indrajit’s chariot and charioteer and put that ogre in duress

Indrajit vanished from the battlefield abruptly much to the monkeys’ delight

But returned invisible and bored Ayodhya’s princes with toxic arrows of might

 

Rama and Lakshmana, struck by the serpentine arrows lay immobilised

The monkeys turned helpless and despondent and stood paralysed

The invisible Indrajit attacked several other monkey chiefs:

He was no stranger to stealth and underhanded mischiefs

Upon seeing his friend Rama and his key army personnel fallen

Sugriva was shocked and sat down in a corner crestfallen

 

Vibhishana looked closely at Rama and grasped the real situation

“O Sugriva, do not grieve. Ask our army not to give way to lamentation 

Rama’s face shows strong signs of life and very soon He will wake

The venom from Indrajit’s arrows has to dissipate—some time it will take.”

Hearing Vibhishana’s reassurances, Sugriva gained confidence

He consoled his folks: “Rama will soon rise and show His competence.”

 

Indrajit became overjoyed seeing Rama and Lakshmana lying prostrate

He assumed they were lifeless and began to celebrate

Rushing to his father’s mansions, he poured forth the day’s events

He said: “Your enemies are no more, father; forget your torments.”

Ravana extolled the valour of his son to the skies

He cried: “Singlehanded, you have exceeded all my other allies.”

 

Ravana then summoned the demonesses guarding Rama’s wife

He said: “Take Sita in my aerial chariot and let her see Rama devoid of life.” 

The ogresses took Sita in the ‘Pushpaka Vimana’ to the battlefield

Sita saw with her own eyes Rama and Lakshmana on arrow beds, congealed

The shocked princess burst into uncontrollable sobs and wondered aloud:

“My father’s expert astrologers predicted I should never be widowed!”

 

“How come their words have proven empty today?

For the death-god my dear husband has become prey.

I saw so many auspicious signals that gave me full hope

They have all turned out false: with this sorrow, I cannot cope.”

Hearing Sita crying bitterly, a kindly demoness ‘Trijata’, consoled her

She said: “Your husband and brother-in-law are alive: this I do infer.”

 

Trijata continued: “This sacred chariot never hovers over death.

Moreover, of hope on the monkeys’ faces, there is no dearth.

Also, I perceive many omens that predict good things for you

Those very same signs bring Lanka’s desolation into my view.”

Hearing the arguments of Trijata, Sita was persuaded

And by the time she was taken back to the Asoka grove, her fears faded

 

Soon, Rama woke from His swoon and found Lakshmana lying still

Agonised, He began wailing aloud and to fight further, He lost the will

That heroic Raghava then told Sugriva: "Without Lakshmana, I shall die

You must leave with your army and escape the ‘rakshasas’ sly."

On hearing Rama's words, the monkeys shed tears copious

They could not bear to see Rama and Lakshmana in situations grievous.

 

Meanwhile, Vibhishana established order in the monkey troops

He then approached Rama, who resembled a flower that droops

That rakshasa proclaimed, "Victory be to Rama" and clove through the crowd

Upon seeing Rama and Lakshmana, he cried, "Alas, fallen are the princes proud!

They look like porcupines with the pierced arrows shining

My brother's son with a perverse soul has brought them down by cunning."

 

Sushena, the monkey with knowledge of medicinal herbs suggested:

"By infusing the juice of celestial plants, life can be injected."

Just then Garuda, the powerful eagle and vehicle of the Supreme arrived

Upon perceiving him, utmost terror the snakes in the area derived

The snake venom that had tied Rama and Lakshmana got neutralised

And the brothers woke as though from a rest, well utilised.

 

Vibhishana and all the monkeys rent the air with their joyous sounds

The fear Ravana and his cronies felt at this tumult knew no bounds

The monkeys romped into Ravana’s city again with uprooted trees and rocks

Desirous of fighting the covert demons, they stormed the place in large flocks

The king of Lanka sent his powerful leaders one by one into the fray

But, the monkeys and bears expelled or killed them all without delay

 

Dhumraksha and Vajradamstra were two reliable ogres Ravana chose

They too died in battle along with their respective armies assembled in rows

Next, Hanuman held Akhampana in combat and had him exterminated 

The powerful Prahasta’s life with his soldiers’, Nila then terminated

Shaken by his many losses, Ravana himself ventured into the warfare

Along with Indrajit, Atikaya, Kumbha, Nikhumba and others, amidst fanfare

 

Rama and his side were more than equal to Ravana and his supporters

The rakshasas had a tough fight once again and retired hurt to their quarters

Rama held the bragging Ravana in combat and of His arrows gave him a taste

The king of Lanka struggled under Rama’s onslaught and was soon laid to waste

To the hurt and swooning Ravana, the magnanimous Rama spoke:

“I do not fight a foe who stands weaponless and broke.”

 

“Return to your palace and come again tomorrow

Once more I shall give you battle and show you many an arrow.”

Ravana, back in his apartments, was steeped in deep worry

He gave commands to wake his giant brother Kumbhakarna in a hurry

Kumbhakarna, had a boon to sleep unhindered, to wake for a day after every half-year

He now slumbered in his palace and resembled a colossus of muscle sheer

 

Kumbhakarna woke up after being roused by several demons

He realised his brother was in trouble and hurried to answer his summons

When Kumbhakarna learnt about Sita’s abduction, he became furious

He rebuked Ravana for his disregard of Vibhishana’s advice glorious

Ravana begged: “What has transpired I cannot undo.

At this point in time, I need help, which only you can do.”

 

Kumbhakarna agreed to help Ravana by going into the war field

“By destroying all your enemies, our nation from calamity I shall shield.”

Making that promise, Kumbhakarna rushed out into the conflict

But, he sensed many bad omens and understood his clan would soon be derelict

However, ignoring the portents, Kumbhakarna trampled everything in his path

He left a trail of destruction, mutilation, death, and blood bath

 

The monkey soldiers frightened out of their wits fled

Angada buoyed them up and soon their perturbation they shed 

Dvivida, Hanuman, Angada and Sugriva engaged Kumbakarna in turn

But, in that ‘rakhsasa’, even the smallest sign of defeat they could not discern

Soon, Kumbhakarna proceeded towards Rama with a mountain peak

But, Rama blew it into bits with His arrows that resembled lightning streaks

 

Rama then decided to end Kumbhakarna’s menace

And began releasing arrows with a quickened pace

He slew Kumbhakarna by slashing off his limbs and head with His deadly darts

That fearsome ogre lay on Lanka’s earth, in a pool of blood and body parts

Ravana heard of his brother’s death and fainted with shock

Coming to, he wailed, “I lost my brother whose loyalty was a rock.”

 

Ravana then sadly contemplated his actions that were buried in sin

He thought: “I should have heeded Vibhishana, the sagacious among all my kin.”

The king of Lanka now realised Rama’s super-human powers

“Rama is the divine being, who the sinful to dust lowers.

I am certain to lose this war, which I waged out of arrogant lust

Alas, I have sacrificed so many! Perish, I too must.”

 

After lamenting a while, Ravana planned for the war to continue 

“We shall fight for you,” said his sons, part of his most accomplished retinue

Trishira, Devantaka, Narantaka, and Atikaya went on an offensive

But, they too were felled by various monkey chiefs, after fights extensive

Ravana and his wives were left to cry over their sons’ bodies

The king of Lanka and his supporters reaped the fruits of depraved follies

 

Indrajit saw his parents mourning his brothers

He vowed: “I shall annihilate Rama by unleashing wonders.”

That capable son of Ravana entered the battle again and there performed a ritual 

The rite made him indomitable and gave him greater powers than usual

Becoming invisible once more, Indrajit flew into the sky

He began to shower weapons like torrential rain from high

 

Rama and Lakshmana fell unconscious, along with several of the monkey troops

Lord Brahma’s missile wrought destruction and struck the soldier groups

Jambhavan instructed Hanuman to fly over to the Himalayas and bring herbs:

‘Mrita Sanjivani, Vishalya Karani, Suvarna Karani and Sandhani’, valuable shrubs

Hanuman flew across the ocean and went North to reach the Himalayan range

Failing to identify the herbs among others, his plan he proceeded to change.

 

“I must uproot and carry back the mountain, which Jambhavan had described

Let the wise bear pick out the medicinal herbs, as he prescribed.”

Thus, Hanuman flew back with the mountain slice to the battleground

With the celestial herbs, Rama and His entire army came around

Rama, Sugriva and Jambhavan then praised Hanuman, the lifesaver

“There is none to equal you: from your duty, you never waver.”

 

The battle resumed again in right earnest and Ravana’s kith entered the fray

Sugriva and Hanuman killed brothers Kumbha and Nikhumba on the same day

Enraged by his setbacks, Ravana ordered Makaraksha, Khara’s son to fight

“Kill Rama and Lakshmana and destroy the monkeys: I thirst for the sight.”

But, Makaraksha too was despatched to the higher regions by Rama’s missile

Good triumphs over evil: trying to change this law of nature is futile

 

Indrajit made a plan: “Let me conjure Sita’s form and slit her throat

Upon learning about it, Rama will be stupefied and cannot stay afloat.”

That sly ‘rakshasa’ came upon Hanuman fighting a large demon troop

Hovering and pulling his sword, he killed Sita’s conjured form with a single swoop

A shocked Hanuman ran back to Rama and reported the horrifying circumstance

Rama, uttering an anguished cry, fainted away and fell to earth in a trance

 

Lakshmana dashed to his brother and placed His head on his lap

“Oh brother, open Your eyes—out of this misery, You must snap.

I shall avenge Sita; and all Your adversaries, I vow to crush

Remember Your duties as a warrior and to the battle, rush.”

Vibhishana came upon Rama and Lakshmana in tears

He said: “O Rama, it is just Indrajit’s trick; wipe away Your fears.”

 

Rama now opened His eyes and listened to Vibhishana’s explanation in detail 

He realised His Sita lived still—He had no reason to wail

Vibhishana then urged Rama to send Lakshmana to fight Indrajit:

“To prevent Indrajit’s sacrifice and exterminate him, Your brother is fit.

The wicked one is in the Nikhumbila cave performing a fire ritual

If he completes it and earns its fruits, no army can be effectual.”

 

Lakshmana rushed to the said cave and challenged Indrajit to combat

He rained arrows upon him and cried, “Today, I vow to flatten you like a gnat.”

Indrajit, forced to abandon his ritual, came out to fight 

“You gave my secret away, vile uncle,” he shouted with spite

Lakshmana and Indrajit then entered into a ferocious mutual attack

Eventually, Lakshmana chopped Indrajit’s head, leaving no room for a comeback

 

Lakshmana returned to Rama victorious, but covered in gashes

Sushena treated his wounds with herbs and prepared him for further clashes

Ravana’s distress knew no end upon learning of Indrajit’s death

The demonesses’ wailing and grief further took away his breath

The king of Lanka cried: “Let me destroy Sita who is the reason for my ruination.”

But, Suparshva, Ravana’s upright minister, prevented him from that errant action.

 

Rama meanwhile, continued fighting the demon forces

Ravana entered the battle arena again to harvest further consequences 

Along with Ravana fought many of his ministers and aides

But none could survive against the monkey and bear brigades

Sugriva eliminated Virupaksha and Mahodara, while Mahaparsva, Angada killed

And at the intensity of the Rama-Ravana combat, the world thrilled

 

On his shoulders, Hanuman carried Rama as He fought

Thus, Rama could strike Ravana sitting on a high perch as he ought

Ravana saw Rama striking him with a calm determination

He schemed to hurt Hanuman and destroy Rama’s concentration

In general, Rama kept His anger under total control

He could command His anger as He pleased, being a tolerant soul

 

So long as the demon was hitting Him, Rama never faltered

But, the moment Ravana struck Hanuman grievously, He was altered

Now, indignation had Rama under its thumb and directed Him:

Rama went and came, as per its commands, grim.

The Lord habitually overlooks any number of personal insults and bother 

But, pardon for the troubles caused to His devotee, He refuses to offer.

 

Presently, Ravana spotted Lakshmana and threw a spear at his bosom

The spear penetrated Lakshmana’s heart and he dropped like a plucked blossom

Rama, incensed, began raining fiery arrows in torrents

Ravana ran for cover to escape their deadly currents

Rama then knelt next His brother and once more felt wracked by despondency

But, Sushena told Rama, “Let not Your mind waver from its constancy.”

 

“Hanuman must once more fetch divine herbs from the Himalayan peak

It will heal Lakshmana and make him sit up and speak.”

Maruti then rushed to Mt. Mahodaya and searched for the plant

Failing to spot it, he knocked and carried back its peak, as though it were an ant

Lakshmana was cured soon and returned to the concluding war

Ravana too came back to fight—and to meet his end, which was not afar.

 

Indra, the chief of the gods of heaven, sent his chariot for Rama to ride

He also sent a spear, some arrows and his charioteer Maatali as guide

The fierce battle between Rama and Ravana resumed

By Rama’s constant stream of sharp arrows, Ravana was consumed

When the demon’s charioteer saw Ravana tottering feebly,

He guided the chariot away from the warfare ably

 

Upon gaining his senses, Ravana asked to re-enter the battle:

That demon king, famed for bravery and resilience, nothing could rattle

In the meanwhile, Sage Agastya arrived there to give Rama advice

He said: “Chant the ‘Aditya Hridaya’ and get energy from the Sun god in a trice.”

Rama did as bid and showered Ravana with His unflinching arrows

The darts slashed the heads of that ogre, who took birth to bestow sorrows

 

But, Ravana’s heads grew back as soon as they were cut

Rama’s efforts were not fructifying, though He rampaged like an elephant in rut

Maatali now spoke: “Aim the missile presided by Brahma at Ravana’s heart.

He will give up his life then, that being his vulnerable part.”

Rama focused His mind and released the powerful missile like a gale

It found its mark and Ravana finally fell to earth, turning pale.

 

The gods and sages who had come to watch the momentous action

Hailed Rama and showered fragrant flowers without cessation

Rama asked Hanuman to meet Sita and convey the news of His victory  

The monkey ran to Janaki’s presence and said, “Upon Rama be glory.

He killed Ravana after a spirited fight and won the war

Your troubles are over now and You may rejoice, O lady without par.”

 

Sita shed incessant tears and said not a word

Hanuman asked: “With tears, why are your eyes blurred?

Have I done any wrong? Why are you so silent?

At this propitious news, I expected you to become vibrant!”

Sita swallowed hard and conveyed her thanks by bringing her palms together

She said: “For all the help you have rendered child, I am grateful forever.”

 

“I am unable to utter freely, because my heart is choked with relief

I am yearning to meet my husband and dispel all my grief.

In this place, away from my home, I cannot gift you any object

Do ask something of me: I wish to show you my respect.”

Hanuman replied: “No need to thank your child and humble servant

Just let me crush the demonesses who tormented You: that’s my wish fervent.”

 

Sita now chided Hanuman for his desire degrading and hasty

She said: “It is Ravana who commanded the demonesses to be nasty

When Vibhishana tells them to decorate me, they will do that too

They merely obey orders, and you must show mercy to them as their due.

Who isn’t a sinner in this world? You, Rama, and I too are sinners.

Your sin is, wanting to kill those who sought your pardon like beggars.

 

Rama, when He wed me, took a vow to never leave my side

But, He did, and for the last ten months, I was here and nearly died

You ask what my sin is? In your hearing, I criticise my husband’s actions 

A wife must never speak to an outsider about her dissatisfactions!

If your master, Rama, were to give deliverance only to those without sin

To take with Him to His abode, Vaikunta, none can He win!"

 

From Sita's sharp response, Hanuman understood the depth of Her forbearance:

He realised that Her lotus feet perpetually generated benevolence.

Once again Sita proved that She was infinitely superior to Her consort

While He protects only His devotees, She is everyone’s impartial resort

‘One Sita equals a thousand Rama’ is no exaggeration 

She is patience personified, beyond even the Lord's comprehension!

 

Mandodari, hearing of Ravana’s death, rushed to the battlefield

“O valiant husband, how much power and fame you did wield?

Even the gods were afraid of going against your wishes

Yet you lie here on bare earth having given up all your riches.

Oh, why did you fail to see Vishnu Himself in Rama’s form?

Why did you covet Sita, the pinnacle of virtues, against every norm?”

 

Sita, the epitome of chaste conduct has the tolerance of Mother earth

And combines the benevolent charm of Lakshmi, the Lord’s consort, in Her birth

She is immersed in Her husband’s thoughts all the time

She has no use for riches, tainted with sins’ grime

When you dared interfere with her, you sealed your fate

Your atrocities that brought this desolation, none can vindicate.

 

Earlier, Rama killed your brother Khara along with thousands of demon folk

He revealed He was no mere mortal by that single stroke

Next, Hanuman came here and destroyed our entire kingdom by setting fire

And mere monkeys built a bridge over the ocean and entered our sphere

Did you not realise Rama is the eternal soul, with neither beginning nor end—

The presiding soul of the universe, whom nothing or none can bend?

 

Rama is the one beyond ignorance; He is Creation’s nourishment

He came to earth for evil’s destruction and Dharma’s establishment

He is the wielder of the Conch, Disc, and Mace

He carries the ‘Srivatsa’ mark on His chest with grace

He is the truly mighty One and is unconquerable

As the Lord of all the worlds, He keeps everything stable

 

O Ravana, my husband, by lust your senses were soiled

Your ignominious ambitions, Rama, the just Lord of all worlds, has foiled."

Mourning thus, Mandodari wept over her husband's slain body

Though she acknowledged, while alive, evil he did embody.

Now, Rama instructed Vibhishana to perform Ravana's final rite:

"Cremate your brother with respect and do what is right."

 

But, Vibhishana refused to do the rituals in Ravana's funeral

Rama advised: "Enmity and hate cease at the end of the carnal."

What He meant was: the hatred in Ravana’s heart had ended

Rama Himself never harbours hatred, because in Him every soul is blended

"If you will not perform the last rites for your brother, let me do it

For many—like Kabandha, Jatayu, and Viradha—funeral pyres I have lit."

 

Hearing Rama's admonition, Vibhishana concluded the solemnities

At Rama's behest, Lakshmana crowned Vibhishana, amidst festivities 

Vibhishana then approached Sita reverently and said: "Rama awaits you.

Please decorate yourself and appear before your husband true." 

Sita replied: "I would like to show Rama my dusty, emaciated state."

But, Vibhishana said: "Rama's wish that you be decked, let us not debate."

 

Sita was soon clad in splendour and carried out in a palanquin 

As she neared Rama, the monkeys jostled one another to see Rama's queen

Rama, seeing Hanuman pushing the monkeys away, felt irritated 

"Let Sita walk and let my devotees see her without being frustrated."

Sita got out of her carriage at once and walked up to Rama slowly

Relief and joy made the dainty lady feel weak and shaky

 

Rama perceived Sita dressed in glorious robes and jewellery 

"Why are you dressed in this grand manner, O Sita?" He asked sternly

Sita, perturbed by Rama's tone said, "Vibhishana conveyed it as Your wish!"

Rama retorted, "You failed to sense my words as mere etiquette: you are foolish.

By defeating the demon that took you away, I salvaged my dynasty's honour

But, accepting you, who lived for long in a stranger's place, would be improper." 

 

"As I look at you, my eyes feel a throbbing strain 

Like the sore eyes of a man, who looks at a light, hurt with pain.

I have done my duty in redeeming you from your offender

You may now go where you please," said Rama to Sita, the one with a waist slender.

Though harsh, Rama placed blame on His own head: this fact His words indicate

After all, the problem is not the light; it is the man in the sore-eyed state.

 

Everyone present there felt appalled and distressed at Rama's attitude

They wondered, "How will the blameless Sita bear this with fortitude?"

Shocked into silence, Sita shed helpless tears of humiliation 

Turning to Lakshmana, she said, "Light me a fire" in indignation 

Sita then spoke quietly to Rama, "O husband, you have insulted me.

When I enter the fire, from Your deplorable doubts, You shall be free."

 

Just then the Gods of heaven appeared and addressed Rama thus:

"You know well that Sita was abducted by force and was helpless

Fully aware of her divine birth and qualities, how could you be so severe?

Why do You stand by without preventing Sita from entering the pyre?

Recollect Your own divinity and the purpose of Your birth:

You and Sita are Narayana and Lakshmi, who pour mercy without dearth."

 

That faultless Sita now proceeded to pray to the God of Fire:

"Prove my chastity by keeping my body intact—my need now is dire."

She then jumped into the flames lit obediently by Lakshmana 

But, the fire God brought her out and handed her to Rama

He said: "Sita is chastity. Your stance is unworthy and unjust.

She is ever faithful to You in thought, word and deed: You know it best!"

 

Rama acknowledged the gods' words and the fire god's statement

He accepted Sita with great delight and contentment

Rama then said: "Sita to me is like sunlight to sun

But, taking her without examining, I, as a King, could not have done.

I know Sita could have destroyed Ravana by mere dint of her virtue

The world too now knows her stature: and Sita, no blame can pursue."

 

Lord Shiva then said to Rama: "O Supreme, seek blessings from Your father.

Look, there he stands with Indra awaiting You, Sita and Your brother."

Rama declared: "I prefer to be addressed not as the Supreme, but as Dasaratha's son.”

Then, prostrating to His father, He said: “Mother Kaikeyi and Bharatha, you must not shun."

Dasaratha, well satisfied, acceded to Rama's request and said:

"Sita and Lakshmana! Serve Rama: that's the path you must always tread."

 

The gods then thanked Rama for completing their mission

Indra said: "O peerless one, ask of us a boon that You envision"

Rama asked, “Let all the monkeys who died battling for me come alive.”

Indra granted the desire and much pleasure did Rama derive

Now the celestials asked Rama to return to Ayodhya and accept its kingship

"Proceed fast: Bharatha, separated from You, has endured enough hardship."

 

Rama took leave of Vibhishana, Sugriva, Hanuman and others

"Thank you all for fighting on my side like my brothers.

O Vibhishana, there's very little time for my exile to end

I must reach Bharatha by then: my stay I cannot extend.

Is there any way to reach my kingdom quickly?

I await your suggestion anxiously."

 

Vibhishana said: "You must travel in ‘Pushpaka’, Ravana's aerial car

Let it be used for a good purpose now: for it, no distance is too far.

All of us want to come with our families and watch Your coronation 

Please take us along, this chariot can assume the required proportion."

So, Rama and Sita, accompanied by all their loving devotees became air bound

Rama showed Sita several spots and their significance, profound

 

When the 'Pushpaka' reached Kishkinda, the monkeys hurriedly got down

They climbed back in with their families, eager to see Rama's head in a crown

Rama soon ordered the aerial car to land at Bharadwaja's hermitage

He enquired about His family's welfare, after prostrating respectfully to the sage

The hermit reassured Him and said, "I saw the events that transpired.

By killing Ravana, You did all the good the righteous required."

 

Rama requested Bharadwaja to make the wild trees produce edible fruits

The monkeys felt satiated feasting on those and other roots and shoots

Bharadwaja now commanded Rama to stay the night at his hut

"Proceed tomorrow morning to Ayodhya; You must not leave me yet."

Rama objected and said: "O sage, you know very well my exile ends tonight

If Bharatha fails to see me at dawn tomorrow, what would be his plight?"

 

The sage replied with a smile: "Send Hanuman ahead with Your message

Let your messenger reassure Bharatha and his anxiety assuage."

Rama said: Hanuman, go to Ayodhya. Tell Bharatha I am at Bharadwaja's place

I shall reach him early tomorrow; with this information, give him solace."

Hanuman, folding his palms and carrying Rama's message, departed

He flew into Ayodhya to calm Bharatha who was growing frustrated.

 

At Nandigrama, Hanuman perceived Bharatha dressed like a hermit

Shatrughna stood close by that devotion's summit

Just then, the day began to dawn and Bharatha looked around sadly

He said, "My Rama has not come to wipe away our melancholy.

O brother Shatrughna, Rama promised to be here the very next day after His exile

Alas, He has not come for some reason, and my waiting has been futile."

 

"I have vowed to shed my life if Rama failed to return 

Please light a fire: I shall jump in it and this body I shall burn.

My eyes have thirsted for my beloved brother's sight for fourteen long years

Yet without mercy for this slave and devotee, He allows me to wallow in tears."

Crying in this manner, that gentle brother of Rama made his decision, dire.

And the peerless Shatrughna lit for Bharatha a large pyre.

 

Hanuman thought, "How do I stop Bharatha from taking this extreme step?

I must sing Rama's story just like I did for Sita: it will hold him like a web!"

That unparalleled monkey then started to chant Rama's name in a sweet voice

He narrated incidents concerning Rama, at which Bharatha began to rejoice

Bharatha and Shatrughna looked around and saw Hanuman appear

They asked eagerly, "More about Rama, we would like to hear."

 

Hanuman folded his palms with respect and said: "I am Rama's slave

I have come here with Rama's message: for His sake, your life you must save.

Rama says: "I am at Bharadwaja's ashram now and will come to you in a short while

Await my return, O Bharatha, my darling brother without guile."

Hearing the words of Hanuman, that pious Bharatha felt overcome

He embraced the divine monkey tightly, like he would his dearest chum.

 

Bharadwaja had a reason for asking Rama to stay a night

He realised Bharatha’s life, sudden joy could shut out and blight

Hanuman became a cushion for Bharatha's intense elation

And kept him sane and alive till Rama returned to tumultuous acclamation

Bharatha and Shatrughna heard the events of the past

And felt delighted by their brothers’ achievements vast.

 

While Ayodhya joyously awaited Rama's arrival and impending coronation 

That divine saviour descended into the kingdom amidst gaiety and celebration 

After embracing His brothers and prostrating to His mothers, Rama stood

He resembled a mighty mountain surmounted by splendorous wood

Kosala's citizens finally felt at peace with all the elements

And busied themselves paying Rama and Sita plentiful compliments

 

Rama and His brothers removed their matted hair and hermit's garb

They were soon clad in outfits appropriate for the Royal orb

Sita was taken to her chambers to change into suitable attire

She too emerged presently, radiant as a bright fire

Rama asked Lakshmana to be His Prince Regent

But Lakshmana declined Rama's offer with a reason cogent

 

"I am already the King of the kingdom of Your servants

What do I care for Ayodhya's sovereignty with its material torments?

There stands Bharatha, who carries out Your every command

You must place in front of that exalted slave of Yours this demand."

Rama only had to look at Bharatha, before he nodded his assent

"Your wish is my command," said that brother, without dissent.

 

Rama with His brothers then climbed a chariot and was taken in a procession

Nine thousand elephants followed, with Sugriva and other monkeys, in unison

At the conclusion of the procession, Vasishta led Rama and Sita to Ayodhya's throne 

From their exalted seat made of precious gems, the divine couple shone.

Vasishta bid the monkeys fetch holy waters from various rivers and oceans

The subsequent scene of Rama’s and Sita's consecration exceeded all notions

 

Sage Vasishta, chanting holy mantras, crowned Rama's divine head

All beings on earth felt as though they had been crowned in Rama's stead:

Rama permeates all, and in Him reside all creation:

In that instant, every creature felt it without exception.

The trees became laden with fragrant flowers and a mild breeze blew

Entire nature smiled in harmony and tranquil everything grew

 

Rama gave away copious gold and jewels to Brahmans and other people

He also gave presents to His friends who were loving and simple:

He presented Sugriva with a gem-studded garland 

And gave Angada an ornament for the arm, a glittering band

Rama gifted to Sita a string of pearls from the wind God 

Sita desiring to give it to Hanuman, looked meaningfully at her Lord.

 

Rama smilingly gave His consent and a few steps Sita walked

Going up to Hanuman and placing the necklace in his hands, she talked:

"Dear Hanuman, You have done me a supreme favour and I am grateful

May your renown grow and May you remain Rama's foremost slave rightful.”

To bless Hanuman, Sita, the goddess of fortune, got down from Her seat 

The glory the monkey acquired then, can anything ever beat?

 

To Vibhishana, Rama gifted the idol He worshipped at prayer

By this action, He again proved His magnanimous fervour

None would part with the deity he worships, easily

But, Lord Rama did that out of His love, unstintingly

Vibhishana thankfully accepted and took the deity—Sri Ranganatha—along

On the way, he put the idol down for rituals—but not for long.

 

After performing his prayers, that statue, Vibhishana tried to pack

But, the deity refused to budge and desired to stay back

The location was between the river ‘Kaveri’s two branches

Set in the southern part of India, Sri Rangam, whose glory is matchless

Vibhishana at once offered to remain back, worshipping the deity

The Lord sent him on to Lanka: but faces South to this day, blessing Vibhishana’s piety. 

 

Under Rama's rule, the world enjoyed peace and bounty

For every being, it was 'Rama, Rama, Rama' in plenty

Rama ruled the kingdom thus for eleven thousand years 

During 'Rama Rajya' all beings were spiritually exalted and lived without fears

Anyone who listens to, reads, tells or writes Rama's divine story

In this world and the next, will obtain the highest glory.
